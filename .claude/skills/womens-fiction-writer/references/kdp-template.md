# KDP Info Document Template — Women's Fiction

Create a companion .docx file for the manuscript with the following sections, providing everything needed to publish on Amazon KDP.

## Required Sections

### Book Title
The full title of the manuscript.

### Subtitle
A subtitle suggestion that hints at the emotional territory and story type (e.g., "A Novel of Second Chances" or "A Story of Family, Secrets, and Starting Over").

### Author
Use the author name from the outline or as specified by the user.

### Book Description
Write a compelling Amazon book description, max 4000 characters. Structure it for women's fiction:

1. **Hook line** — one emotionally resonant sentence that establishes the protagonist's situation and the emotional stakes
2. **The protagonist** — who she is, what her life looks like, what she thinks she wants
3. **The disruption** — what forces change (a death, a discovery, a daughter's call, a divorce)
4. **The journey** — what she must face, who she must reckon with, what she might lose
5. **The question** — what she must answer to find her way through (frame as an emotional question, not a plot question)
6. **Closing hook** — a line that makes readers feel they need to know how she comes through

Use HTML-compatible formatting (bold for emphasis) since Amazon supports it. Weave in relevant keywords naturally. Avoid spoiling the ending.

Women's fiction descriptions should feel emotionally warm and intelligent — not thriller-tense. The tone should signal "this is a book for readers who want to feel something real."

### Amazon Keywords (7 phrases)
Seven keyword phrases optimized for Amazon search. Mix broad and specific:
- 2-3 broad women's fiction terms (e.g., "women's fiction novels", "book club reads", "upmarket fiction")
- 2-3 theme/situation specific (e.g., "midlife reinvention novel", "mother daughter relationship books", "grief and healing fiction")
- 1-2 audience/mood specific (e.g., "emotional women's fiction", "hopeful literary fiction")

### BISAC Categories (3 suggestions)
Three BISAC category codes with full names. Women's fiction categories to consider:
- FICTION / Women
- FICTION / Family Life / General
- FICTION / Family Life / Siblings
- FICTION / Family Life / Marriage & Divorce
- FICTION / Friendship
- FICTION / Coming of Age
- FICTION / Literary
- FICTION / Small Town & Rural
- FICTION / Psychological

Always include the most specific category available.

### Suggested Price
- Ebook: $3.99-$5.99 (novellas lower end, full novels higher)
- Paperback: $14.99-$17.99
- Note on KDP Select / Kindle Unlimited eligibility (note: women's fiction often performs well wide, not just in KU)

### Series Information
- Whether this is a standalone or has series potential
- If series potential: what would connect the books? (Same protagonist, same town, same ensemble of characters?)
- Brief notes on potential future books

### Comparable Titles
3-5 real, published women's fiction books with genuine market overlap. Include:
- Title and author
- Why it's comparable (protagonist type, themes, tone, audience)
- Publication date (ideally within last 3 years for relevance)

Examples of the kind of comp titles to look for: Jojo Moyes, Liane Moriarty, Taylor Jenkins Reid, Emily Henry (when writing women's fiction), Ann Napolitano, Kristin Hannah, Lisa Jewell, Lucy Foley (when women's fiction vs. thriller).

### Target Audience
Describe the ideal reader:
- Age range (women's fiction skews 35-65, but can be broader depending on protagonist age)
- Reading preferences and habits (book clubs? Literary fiction? Commercial fiction crossover?)
- What emotional experience they're seeking
- Which authors they likely enjoy

### Cover Image Prompt
A detailed, ready-to-use prompt for AI image generation (Midjourney, DALL-E, Ideogram, etc.) describing the ideal cover.

**The prompt must include:**

1. **The exact book title** rendered as text on the cover
2. **A placeholder for the author name** at the bottom
3. **Visual style** matching current women's fiction market trends:
   - Upmarket/book club fiction: Painterly or photographic, warm palette, often featuring a woman, a place, or an object with emotional resonance
   - Midlife reinvention: Often features a woman (usually seen from behind or in profile, sometimes running or standing in a significant location)
   - Family saga: Often features a house, a landscape, or multiple female figures
   - Grief & healing: Quieter, more introspective imagery — water, light, open spaces
   - Sisterhood: Two or more women, warm and intimate
4. **Scene or visual description** specific to THIS book's emotional core
5. **Composition notes:**
   - Title placement (usually top third or center)
   - Author name at bottom
   - Enough negative space for text readability
   - Vertical/portrait orientation (1600x2560px)
   - Avoid covers that look like romance (couples, clinches) unless the book has significant romantic content

## Formatting

Use the same docx generation approach as the manuscript, but simpler:
- US Letter, 1 inch margins
- Section headings as Heading 1
- Body text in 12pt Times New Roman
- Single spacing is fine for this document
