# Holiday Romance Author: 6-Phase Workflow

## Phase 1: Outline Intake & Verification

**Objective:** Confirm you have a complete, usable outline and identify holiday-specific requirements.

**What I Check:**
- All chapters are listed with scene breakdowns (not just chapter names)
- Holiday is clearly identified (Christmas, New Year's Eve, Halloween, Valentine's Day, Thanksgiving, Fourth of July, etc.)
- Relationship type noted (MF/FF/MM/Multi/Non-binary)
- Heat level indicated (sweet/moderate/steamy/scorching)
- Main conflict and resolution arc are clear
- Holiday Integration Engine is visible (holiday actively shapes plot, not just decoration)

**If Outline is Incomplete:**
- Missing scenes? I ask for a holiday romance outline builder first
- Holiday unclear? I ask which specific holiday and what date range the story covers
- Relationship/heat level ambiguous? I confirm before proceeding

**Red Flags That Pause the Process:**
- Holiday is mentioned but not integrated into plot ("They met at Christmas and fell in love" without the holiday forcing encounters, creating obstacles, or driving the climax)
- Scenes exist but without holiday context (generic restaurant meet-cute instead of "they collide while shopping for holiday decorations")
- Outline treats holiday as decoration, not engine

**Deliverable:** Verified outline with holiday integration confirmed.

## Phase 2: Interactive Setup (8 Questions)

**Objective:** Gather specifications for prose style, romance intensity, and holiday-specific preferences.

**The 8 Questions:**

1. **Holiday & Date Range**
   - Which specific holiday? (Christmas, Hanukkah, New Year's Eve, Valentine's Day, Halloween, Thanksgiving, Fourth of July, Easter, Cinco de Mayo, other)
   - Story date range? (e.g., "November 15 - December 25" for Christmas)
   - This ensures holiday sensory details and timeline are accurate

2. **Subgenre**
   - Contemporary, small-town, second-chance, workplace, stranded/forced proximity, found family, paranormal, fantasy
   - Subgenre determines tone and obstacle types

3. **Heat Level**
   - Sweet (fade-to-black, closed-door)
   - Moderate (on-page kissing, sensual tension)
   - Steamy (explicit scenes, detailed intimacy)
   - Scorching (multiple explicit scenes, intensity)
   - Heat level shapes prose detail in intimate moments

4. **Relationship Type & POV**
   - MF / FF / MM / Multi / Non-binary
   - First person alternating / Third person alternating / Single POV
   - POV choice affects internal monologue style

5. **Tense**
   - Present or Past
   - Holiday romance often feels more immediate in present tense

6. **Target Word Count**
   - Novella (20K-50K)
   - Standard (70K-90K)
   - Extended (90K-130K)
   - This determines chapter count and average chapter length

7. **Holiday Sensory Palette Customization** (optional)
   - "I want a more rural Christmas with farm elements"
   - "This is an autumn holiday in a city, not a small town"
   - Custom details I weave throughout

8. **Any Specific Requests**
   - Avoid certain tropes?
   - Specific character professions or settings?
   - Known names to avoid beyond the standard list?
   - These get logged in the editorial process

**Deliverable:** Setup sheet with all 8 confirmed and holiday-specific details locked in.

## Phase 3: Pre-Writing Preparation

**Objective:** Load all guardrails, build tracking tools, confirm holiday authenticity framework.

**What I Build:**

1. **Names Avoidance List Check**
   - Load built-in list of overused romance names (40+ generic names)
   - Load holiday-specific overused names (Holly, Noelle, Joy, Faith, Hope, Nick/Nicholas for Christmas; Autumn, Raven for Halloween; etc.)
   - Cross-reference all character names in outline
   - Flag any matches; plan replacements

2. **Holiday Sensory Palette**
   - Build comprehensive sensory list for THIS specific holiday
   - Example for Christmas: pine, cinnamon, woodsmoke, cold air on warm skin, twinkling lights, crunch of snow, mulled wine, ginger, frost on windows, silent night, etc.
   - Example for Halloween: wet leaves, candle wax, fog, apple cider, crisp night air, pumpkin spice, costume fabrics, jack-o'-lantern glow, harvest moon, etc.
   - This palette gets referenced in every chapter

3. **Running Holiday Motif Tracker**
   - Table with: Motif Name | Introduced Chapter | Evolution | Callback Chapters | Resolution
   - Tracks recurring holiday details that evolve: the countdown calendar, the holiday playlist, the family tradition, the holiday food being prepared, the decorations, etc.
   - Ensures subtle repetition without repetition feeling forced

4. **Holiday Integration Engine Checklist**
   - For every chapter, I'll verify:
     - Rule 1 (Holiday as Plot Engine): Holiday actively shapes what happens
     - Rule 2 (Holiday Deadline): Urgency toward the holiday moment
     - Rule 3 (Seasonal Emotional Resonance): Sensory details tied to emotion
     - Rule 4 (Holiday-Specific Obstacles): Problems feel organic to this holiday
     - Rule 5 (Holiday Resolution): Climax uses the holiday for emotional impact

5. **Output Folder Setup**
   - Create working directory for manuscript and metadata files
   - Set up file naming convention (Author-HolidayRomance-Draft.docx, etc.)

**Deliverable:** All guardrails loaded, tracking tools built, holiday framework confirmed ready.

## Phase 4: Manuscript Writing (Chapter by Chapter)

**Objective:** Write every chapter with Holiday Integration Engine rules applied immediately after each chapter.

**For Each Chapter:**

1. **Write Full Chapter**
   - Follow outline's scene breakdown
   - No skipped scenes
   - No placeholders
   - Write at target word count (1,000-2,500 for novella chapters; 2,500-4,000 for standard; 3,500-5,000 for extended)

2. **Immediately Apply Holiday Integration Engine (5-Rule Check)**
   - **Rule 1 Check:** Does the holiday actively shape what happens in this chapter? (Forced proximity due to weather? Meet-cute during holiday shopping? Character obligation from holiday prep driving the scene?)
   - **Rule 2 Check:** Is there urgency toward the holiday moment? Do characters reference time passing? ("Only 3 days until Christmas," "Halloween is in a week," etc.)
   - **Rule 3 Check:** Are sensory details tied to emotional beats? When character is vulnerable, are holiday sensations amplifying that? When conflict rises, does the holiday atmosphere create irony or contrast?
   - **Rule 4 Check:** Are obstacles organic to this holiday? (Weather, family obligations, event preparations, traditions) Not generic problems with holiday costumes.
   - **Rule 5 Check (climax chapters only):** Does the climax use the holiday for maximum emotional impact?

3. **Check Prose Craft Rules 1-10**
   - Sensory immersion by holiday? (Can I taste the cinnamon? Feel the October chill?)
   - Holiday atmosphere mirroring emotion?
   - Ticking clock references?
   - Traditions building intimacy?
   - Holiday food/drink as love language?
   - Weather used as romance tool?
   - Community/belonging feeling?
   - Holiday memories in internal monologue?
   - Grand gesture tied to holiday?
   - Pacing matching holiday anticipation?

4. **Update Running Holiday Motif Tracker**
   - Log which motifs appear in this chapter
   - Note evolution (the countdown moves forward, decorations shift, traditions deepen)
   - Confirm callbacks work

5. **Apply Guardrails**
   - No names matching avoidance list (already verified)
   - No AI-ism crutches slipping in ("a sense of," "couldn't help but," "magic of [holiday]," etc.)
   - Character voices distinct and consistent

6. **Move to Next Chapter**
   - Complete write → check rules → update trackers → next

**Logging:** After each chapter, log:
- Chapter number, title, word count
- Holiday Integration Engine compliance (all 5 rules: yes/no with notes)
- Prose craft issues caught and fixed
- Motif updates
- Any guardrail interventions

**No Rushing:** Every chapter gets the full treatment. For a 70K standard novel (28 chapters at ~2,500 words each), this phase takes 5-8 hours of focused writing and verification.

**Deliverable:** Complete manuscript with every chapter verified against Holiday Integration Engine and prose craft rules.

## Phase 5: Editorial Pipeline (5 Stages)

**Objective:** Revise the manuscript through a rigorous 5-stage process, eliminating AI patterns and ensuring holiday romance authenticity.

### Stage 1: AI-ism Scrub

**Target:** Remove 30 AI writing crutches — 15 universal + 15 holiday-romance-specific.

**Universal Crutches (strike from everywhere):**
- "a sense of"
- "couldn't help but"
- "something shifted"
- "the air between them"
- "a flutter in her stomach"
- "his gaze darkened"
- "she gasped"
- "need pooled low"
- "it was like"
- "suddenly"
- "electricity between them"
- "chemistry that sparked"
- "without thinking"
- "barely resisting"
- "a wave of"

**Holiday-Romance-Specific Crutches (strike from everywhere):**
- "the magic of [holiday]"
- "like something out of a Hallmark movie"
- "holiday spirit"
- "festive atmosphere"
- "twinkle in their eye"
- "winter wonderland"
- "love was the best gift of all"
- "the real meaning of [holiday]"
- "miracle"
- "holiday cheer filled the air"
- "decked out in holiday finery"
- "cozy warmth spread through them"
- "the spirit of the season"
- "magic was in the air"
- "it was the best [holiday] ever"

**Process:** Full read-through. Every instance replaced with specific, sensory, character-grounded writing. This usually reveals 40-80 instances across a novel.

**Quality Check:** After removal, read passages aloud. Do they sound like actual people? Or is the replacement still awkward? Fix rhythm.

### Stage 2: Developmental Edit

**Target:** Structural assessment across 10 dimensions specific to holiday romance.

**The 10 Dimensions:**

1. **Holiday Integration Compliance** — All 5 rules consistently applied? Any chapters where holiday disappears? Any "just decorative" holiday details?
2. **Deadline Urgency Arc** — Does pressure build toward the holiday? Or is the countdown forgettable?
3. **Sensory Palette Consistency** — Are sensory details from the established palette present in every chapter? Or do some feel generic?
4. **Obstacle Authenticity** — Are all obstacles organic to this specific holiday? Any that feel like "romance obstacles that happen to be set during a holiday"?
5. **Romantic Tension vs. Holiday Tension** — Are these two threads pulling together or fighting each other?
6. **Character Voice Consistency** — Do both leads have distinct internal monologues that remain consistent? (Common problem: voices blend in the middle)
7. **Emotional Escalation** — Does emotional investment rise with the romantic relationship AND with the approaching holiday?
8. **Pacing Against Holiday Arc** — Do early chapters feel like planning/preparation? Middle chapters like rising complications? Final chapters like the holiday itself (fast, intense, climactic)?
9. **Community Feeling** — For holiday romances, the setting/community should feel like a world reader wants to inhabit. Is it vivid? Or is it generic backdrop?
10. **Grand Gesture Authenticity** — Does the climactic gesture feel earned and tied to this specific holiday? Or does it feel tacked on?

**Output:** Revision memo identifying top 3 structural issues with specific chapters and fix recommendations.

### Stage 3: Holiday Authenticity Check

**Target:** Verify holiday details are accurate, culturally sensitive, and seasonally appropriate.

**Accuracy Checks:**
- Weather/daylight/seasonal timing correct for the date range? (Can't have May snow in a July Fourth romance; can't have outdoor Christmas activities in Australia without summer-specific changes)
- Holidays observed with cultural accuracy? (If it's Hanukkah, are the eight days and traditions portrayed correctly? If it's Diwali, is the festival represented authentically?)
- Food/traditions authentic? (Don't have typical "Christmas dinner" if the story is set in a culture with different holiday foods)
- Regional variations respected? (Rural Christmas ≠ urban Christmas ≠ small-town Christmas)

**Cultural Sensitivity Review:**
- Non-Christian holidays portrayed with respect, not exoticism
- No stereotypes about how communities celebrate
- If characters are celebrating a holiday that's not their culture of origin, is that choice meaningful/explained?

**Seasonal Authenticity:**
- Clothing choices accurate for season?
- Event timing plausible? (Don't schedule a massive parade for October 30 if it's Halloween-adjacent)
- Daylight/darkness accurate? (Late December = fewer daylight hours; early July = longer days)

**Output:** Authenticity checklist signed off. Any corrections applied.

### Stage 4: Beta Read Simulation

**Target:** Reader satisfaction check. Rate as a holiday romance reader.

**Calibration:** I read as a holiday romance reader expecting:
- Immersive holiday atmosphere (can I FEEL the holiday?)
- Organic obstacles that feel like THIS holiday's problems
- Romance that earns its ending
- Holiday serves story, not story serves holiday
- Both leads are complex, not cardboard
- Emotional payoff at the climax
- Sense of belonging/community
- Heat level matches what was promised
- Distinct voice and tone

**Feedback I Generate:**
- 3 things that work really well (what lands emotionally)
- 3 things that could be stronger (pacing, character depth, holiday integration)
- 1-2 reader satisfaction questions (Does the climax feel inevitable? Does the holiday moment feel earned?)

**Output:** Beta reader memo with honest feedback.

### Stage 5: Targeted Revision (Top 3 Fixes)

**Target:** Apply the top 3 structural improvements identified in stages 2-4.

**Example fixes might include:**
- Strengthen holiday deadline urgency (add more time markers in Act 1-2)
- Deepen community feeling (add secondary character moments that build world)
- Amplify sensory immersion (weave in more specific holiday sensory details)
- Resolve pacing issue in Act 2 (tighten scenes, deepen internal monologue)
- Strengthen grand gesture authenticity (tie it more specifically to a tradition/detail established earlier)

**Process:** Targeted rewriting. Not a full rewrite, but strategic improvements to the identified top 3 issues.

**Quality Assurance:** Re-read for unintended side effects. Did the fix create a new problem? Does prose flow naturally?

**Deliverable:** Final, polished manuscript ready for formatting and KDP submission.

## Phase 6: Delivery & KDP Metadata

**Objective:** Generate final manuscript and KDP metadata file.

### Manuscript Deliverable (.docx)

**Formatting Standards:**
- Font: Times New Roman 12pt
- Line spacing: 1.5
- Paragraph indent: 0.5" first line
- Page breaks between chapters
- Standard formatting for title page, copyright page, chapter headers, scene breaks, back matter

**Contents:**
- Title page (title, author/pen name, byline)
- Copyright page (copyright year, publisher info, standard copyright text)
- Table of Contents (all chapters listed with page numbers)
- Main text (all chapters in order with scene breaks)
- Back matter (author bio, series info if applicable, call-to-action for next book)
- Ready to upload to KDP or send to professional formatter

### KDP Metadata Deliverable (.docx)

**Contains:**
- **Title & Subtitle** — Holiday romance titles should signal the holiday (e.g., "A Christmas Reunion," "Halloween Hearts," "New Year's Second Chance")
- **Series Info** (if applicable) — Series name, book number, series description
- **7 Holiday-Romance-Specific Keywords** — Optimized for seasonal search (e.g., "holiday romance," "Christmas romance," "second-chance romance," "small town romance," "forced proximity," "contemporary romance," "sweet romance" — or adjusted for heat level)
- **BISAC Categories** — 2-3 categories (e.g., FICTION / Romance / Contemporary, FICTION / Romance / Holidays)
- **Back-Cover Copy** — 150-200 words, holiday-focused hook
- **Comparable Titles** — 3-5 recent holiday romance bestsellers or comp titles (Amazon categories to check)
- **Cover Direction** — Visual brief for cover designer (holiday imagery, mood, tone suggestions)
- **Author Bio** — 100-150 words (pen name, author platform, books published)
- **Launch Timing Guidance** — When to release for THIS holiday (e.g., Christmas romance ready by November 1 for holiday season)

**Output:** KDP metadata .docx file, ready to copy-paste into KDP dashboard.

## Timeline Expectations

- **Novella (20K-50K):** 2-4 hours total (shorter chapters, fewer editorial passes)
- **Standard (70K-90K):** 5-8 hours total (most common length, full editorial pipeline)
- **Extended (90K-130K):** 8-12 hours total (longer manuscript, deeper editorial investment)

Timeline includes all 6 phases: intake, setup, prep, writing, editorial, delivery.
