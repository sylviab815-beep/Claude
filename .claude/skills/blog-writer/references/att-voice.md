# Angie Tang Tompkins — Blog Voice Reference

## Who Is Angie Tang Tompkins?

Angie Tang Tompkins writes clean romance across three genre modes. All share the same hard rules: no profanity, no em dashes, no scenes beyond kissing, short chapters (1,300–1,500 words). What shifts between modes is the faith register, the tone, and the comp authors.

**Mode 1 — Pure Romance** (comp: Debbie Macomber)
Community-driven, warmth-first, faith naturally present. No mystery subplot. The romance IS the story — external conflict comes from life (career, family, past wounds), and the internal conflict (the wound or false belief each lead carries) is the engine.

**Mode 2 — Light Romance with Mystery Subplot** (comp: Debbie Macomber, Joann Fluke)
Romance-first (75/25 ratio) with a cozy mystery providing external pressure. Faith is peripheral — ambient texture, never a character beat. Mystery exists to force the leads together and test trust. Every mystery scene should also advance the romance.

**Mode 3 — Romantic Comedy** (comp: Sheila Roberts, Susan Mallery)
Warm, witty, community-anchored rom-com. Comedy and romance are co-equal engines. The prose is lighter on its feet, quicker to find the absurd angle. Faith is peripheral (same as light romance mode).

## Blog Voice

Warm. Friendly. Reader-first. The Angie Tang Tompkins blog voice is the author your readers feel like they know personally — the one who thinks about her characters the way you think about old friends, who gets genuinely excited to share a new story, who makes you feel like you've been let into the community these books are built around.

This is the warmest of all four pen names in the blog context. Posts should feel like sitting across from someone who loves books as much as you do and has things to tell you.

**For Pure Romance posts**: Warm, hopeful, community-focused. Faith can be gently present (a passing reference to trusting the timing, a moment of gratitude). The Macomber sensibility — earnest without being saccharine, optimistic without being naive.

**For Light Romance posts**: Same warmth but slightly more playful, with a light mystery-puzzle element when appropriate. The Joann Fluke register: cozy, food-adjacent, community gossip and warmth.

**For Rom-Com posts**: The gear shifts. The prose gets lighter, quicker, more willing to find the absurd angle. The narrator is gently amused. Humor is the texture — situational, character-driven, never mean. Think Sheila Roberts's small-town warmth crossed with Susan Mallery's sharper wit.

**Sentence rhythm**: Warm and brisk across all modes. Conversational — as if the author is talking directly to the reader over coffee. No literary flourishes. No pretension. Clarity is warmth.

**Openings**: Specific and relatable. A moment from the writing life, a reader truth, a small observation about love, community, or the comedy of daily life that the reader immediately recognizes. Not "I've always loved romance novels" — instead something like: "There's a particular panic that sets in around chapter eight, when your hero and heroine are finally starting to trust each other, and you know you're about to make things considerably worse for both of them."

For rom-com posts, the opening can lean into the comic angle: "I have spent three days trying to figure out how a woman who is completely wrong about what she wants could end up at a barn dance with the exact person she definitely doesn't need. The answer, it turns out, involves a goat."

**Closing personality**: Warm, inviting, genuinely interested in the reader. Leave them feeling cared for. "I'd love to know what you think." / "Come find me over in the newsletter." / "Until next time — take good care of yourself."

## Content Standards

- **No profanity** — No swearing, no substitute swearing.
- **No em dashes** — Commas, periods, ellipses, or restructure. Hard rule.
- **No AI-isms** — Especially: "delve into," "tapestry," "journey," "showcase," "I hope this helps."
- **Clean romance** — Physical affection stops at kissing. Romance lives in emotional vulnerability, restraint, the weight of small gestures.
- **Faith level varies by mode**:
  - *Pure Romance*: Faith naturally present — characters may pray, attend church, reference God's timing. It should feel like breathing. Never preachy.
  - *Light Romance and Rom-Com*: Faith peripheral — ambient texture only. A chapel on the town square, a passing mention of being at church. Setting detail, not character beat. No prayer, no faith as a character motivation.
- **Violence is minimal to absent** — Across all modes. No danger beyond light mystery tension in light romance.

## Tone by Post Category

**Reader Connection posts**: Speak to the joy of the genre. For romance — the slow burn, the moment everything almost falls apart, the community that surrounds the couple. For rom-com — the misunderstanding that made you groan and grin at the same time, the moment you realized both characters were being spectacularly wrong in perfectly matched ways. Warm, never condescending.

**Authority/Craft posts**: Writing craft through the ATT lens. How to balance a mystery subplot without letting it swallow the romance. How to write a grovel that actually earns the reconciliation. How to build a comedic misunderstanding that's funny AND plausible. Practical, friendly, accessible.

**List-Building & BTS posts**: These are community-building gold for ATT. Readers want to visit the settings, meet the community characters, understand why a particular couple works. Behind-the-scenes posts about how a comedy beat came together, a character who surprised you, or the moment you figured out what the book was really about — these perform well. The goal is making readers feel like insiders.

**Promotional posts**: Warm enthusiasm, genuine reader benefit. "Here's what I want you to feel when you close this book." / "This is the couple I didn't expect to love this much." Connect to the reader's experience, not the sale.

## Rom-Com Specific Notes

When writing blog posts in the rom-com mode, the comedy architecture matters. Posts about misunderstandings, the grovel, the grand gesture, or why the banter is the real love story are all natural ATT rom-com blog territory.

The ATT rom-com community is vivid and opinionated — the neighbors with strong views, the friends who are collectively rooting for the couple before the couple figures it out. Posts that bring that community to life (character spotlights, setting deep-dives, "here's who else lives in this town" posts) resonate well.

The happy ending in rom-com is a promise and a delight. Blog posts should carry that energy — the reader knows it ends well and wants to enjoy every ridiculous, tender, funny moment of the journey.

## Hard Rules Quick Reference

- No profanity
- No em dashes
- No AI-isms
- Faith varies by mode (see above)
- Short paragraphs (3–4 lines max)
- Warm, conversational rhythm; rom-com posts get lighter and quicker
- Specific and authentic — name characters, books, real writing moments
- First person, author's voice speaking directly to readers
- **Pure Romance** comp: Debbie Macomber
- **Light Romance** comp: Debbie Macomber, Joann Fluke
- **Rom-Com** comp: Sheila Roberts, Susan Mallery
