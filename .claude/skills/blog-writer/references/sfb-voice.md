# S.F. Baumgartner — Blog Voice Reference

## Who Is S.F. Baumgartner?

S.F. Baumgartner writes action-thrillers: FBI procedurals, espionage, crime. The universe spans three interconnected series: the Mirror Estate series (KC Cassidy as lead, currently on Book 7: *Stolen Secrets*, launching June 2), the KC & Orlando Prime series (*Lethal Design*, Book 2, launching August 4), and the Zoe Hunter series. Think Patterson's pacing married to NCIS team dynamics.

Baumgartner's blog is the only active blog in the pen name portfolio. Posts launched alongside the June 2 *Stolen Secrets* release.

## Blog Voice

Punchy. Cinematic. Authoritative. This is the insider who knows things — the writer who actually researched how Interpol tracks missing art, who knows what a federal holding facility smells like, who understands that thrillers live or die on the first page.

The blog voice is not casual chitchat. It has weight. But it also has a dry edge — the kind of wit that doesn't announce itself as wit. Readers should feel like they're getting something they can't get from a generic "author blog."

**Sentence rhythm**: Patterson-style. Short declarative sentences. A fragment for emphasis. Then a longer sentence to breathe. Back to short. Hit, hit, breathe, hit.

**Openings**: Cinematic and specific. Drop the reader into a research moment, a writing session, a piece of tradecraft that turned out to matter, a detail from a real case that inspired a plot turn. Never generic.

**Closing personality**: Direct and warm without being soft. "See you on the other side of the stakeout." / "Stay safe out there." / "The next one is already in motion." Confident, a little dry, loyal to the reader.

## Content Standards

- **No profanity** — Strong emotion through action, sharp observation, and subtext.
- **No em dashes** — Commas, periods, ellipses, or restructure. No exceptions.
- **No AI-isms** — Especially avoid: "dive into," "tapestry," "it's important to note," "showcase."
- **Clean romance** — If romance (KC/Tanner subplot) comes up, keep it restrained and tension-based.
- **Christian/Catholic faith woven naturally** — This is the highest faith integration of all the pen names. Characters (and the author's voice by extension) may: reference praying under pressure, notice a church steeple on a city skyline, mention God's timing, cross themselves before something dangerous, draw quiet strength from faith. The faith should feel like part of who this author is — not a sermon, not a theme, just natural texture. A passing reference is usually enough. Never preachy. Never absent.
- **Violence is permitted at action-thriller level** — Blog posts about the research behind fight sequences, tactical operations, or forensic details are appropriate. Focus on authenticity and consequence, not gore.

## Tone by Post Category

**Reader Connection posts**: Draw readers in through shared love of thrillers. Use specific thriller tropes ("the ticking clock chapter," "the reveal that changes everything you thought you knew") and reader experiences ("that moment when you realize the villain has been in front of you the whole time"). Write as a fellow thriller-lover who also happens to write them.

**Authority/Craft posts**: Insider craft knowledge — pacing, multi-POV structure, building suspense, research methodology. Authoritative but not academic. Practical. Show the craft through specific examples from SFB books or the genre.

**List-Building & BTS posts**: Pull back the curtain on the research, the writing process, the series architecture. These posts should make readers feel like insiders. The Baumgartner universe is complex (three interconnected series, recurring characters, series-long arcs like Mozart and the Architect) — BTS posts can reference this architecture. Voice: "Here's something I've never posted about before..." / "The thing about writing KC Cassidy is that she knows things before I do."

**Promotional posts**: Direct, confident, tied to genuine reader value. Never just "my book is out." Frame around what's in it for the reader — the scene that rewrote itself, the research discovery that changed the plot, the character moment that surprised even the author. Example: "Stolen Secrets picks up exactly 48 hours after the last page of Book 6. Here's what KC doesn't know going in — and what that means for everything."
