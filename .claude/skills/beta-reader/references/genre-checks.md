# Genre-Specific Beta Read Checks

Find the section matching the user's genre and apply those checks in addition to the universal ones.

---

## Romance (All Subgenres)

- Is the chemistry believable? Do I WANT these people together?
- Does the conflict feel real and rooted in character, or manufactured to create obstacles?
- Is the black moment devastating enough — do I genuinely believe they might not make it?
- Does the HEA/HFN feel earned, or does it arrive too easily?
- Is the heat level consistent with genre expectations (and with what the author specified)?
- Is the love interest present, mentioned, or on the narrator's mind in every chapter?
- Could you remove the romance and still have a complete story? (You shouldn't be able to.)

### Romantic Comedy specifically:
- Is it actually funny? Does every chapter have moments of humor?
- Is the narrator's voice comedic — or does it read like a therapy journal with occasional jokes?
- Are there set pieces and running gags, or just situational awkwardness?

### Romantic Suspense specifically:
- Is the external threat escalating consistently?
- Is the danger real or does it feel like wallpaper?
- Does the suspense thread integrate with the romance, or do they feel like separate books?

### Dark Romance specifically:
- Is the darkness earned, consistent, and within the stated content boundaries?
- Does the morally gray hero have internal logic — do I understand him even if I don't approve?
- Is the intensity maintained throughout, or does it go soft mid-book?

### Historical Romance specifically:
- Are period details accurate and woven in naturally, or info-dumped?
- Does the dialogue feel authentic to the period without being impenetrable?
- Is the slow burn paced for the genre expectations?

---

## Women's Fiction

- Is the protagonist's inner transformation the central engine of the book?
- Can I trace her wound → resistance → cracks → shift → transformation across the story?
- Is the transformation earned and paced — or does it arrive suddenly at the end?
- Does the ending feel honest and credible, or too tidy?
- Are the ensemble relationships emotionally specific and distinct?
- Is subtext doing work — or are characters explaining their feelings too directly?
- Are themes emerging naturally from events, or being stated outright?
- Does the book deliver what its specific women's fiction category promises (midlife reinvention, grief & healing, family saga, sisterhood, etc.)?

---

## Cozy Mystery

- Is the body discovered early enough (by chapter 2-3)?
- Is the sleuth likable and competent without being perfect?
- Are there enough suspects — and are they all plausible?
- Are clues planted fairly — could a careful reader solve it?
- Are red herrings convincing, or obviously wrong?
- Is the cozy world (setting, food, community) warm and inviting?
- Is the mystery central, or does it disappear for long stretches?
- Does the revelation feel satisfying and logical — or does it come out of nowhere?
- Is the killer treated the same as other suspects before the reveal (no unequal narrative attention)?

---

## Thriller / Suspense

- Is the tension escalating consistently — or are there long flat stretches?
- Is the pacing relentless enough for the genre?
- Are twists earned — set up properly in advance — or cheap and random?
- Are the stakes high enough and real enough to keep me scared?
- Does the resolution satisfy — does it close the loop it opened?
- Is the protagonist active — making choices — or passive — reacting to events?
- Does the threat feel credible and specific, or vague and generic?

---

## Mystery (Non-Cozy)

- Are clues planted fairly throughout — could I have solved it?
- Is the solution satisfying and logical when revealed?
- Are red herrings effective without being frustrating?
- Is the detective/investigator a compelling POV to be in?
- Is the pacing right — tension building toward the reveal?
- Does the ending close all the threads it opened?

---

## Fantasy / Paranormal

- Is the worldbuilding clear without being a data dump in the opening chapters?
- Are the magic/power rules consistent — do they feel like rules, not plot conveniences?
- Do the stakes feel real — is there genuine risk?
- Is the world integrated into the story, or just set dressing behind a contemporary plot?
- Do the fantastical elements create complications rather than solving them too easily?
- Is the reader given enough to understand the world without stopping the story?

### Paranormal Romance specifically:
- Does the paranormal element add genuine conflict to the romance?
- Are the rules consistent enough that I trust the author knows what they are?

---

## Literary / General / Upmarket Fiction

- Is the prose doing enough heavy lifting — is the writing itself earning its place?
- Are the themes emerging naturally from events and character, or being stated outright?
- Is the character development compelling enough to carry the story?
- Is the pacing deliberate and intentional — or does it drag?
- Does the ending feel earned and honest — not neat, but true?
- Is there enough external tension to keep pages turning alongside the internal journey?
- Does the voice feel distinct and specific — or generic "literary" voice?

---

## Young Adult (Any Genre)

- Is the protagonist's voice genuinely YA — or does it sound like an adult writing a teen?
- Are the stakes high enough for the protagonist's age and world?
- Is the coming-of-age element present and driving the story?
- Is the pacing right for the audience (YA tends to move faster than adult fiction)?
- Are the relationships (friendship, first love, family) emotionally resonant?
- Does the book respect the intelligence of its young reader?
