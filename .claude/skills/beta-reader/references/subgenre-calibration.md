# Subgenre Calibration — Don't Flag These as Problems

Before flagging anything as a weakness, check this list. These are genre features, not bugs.

---

## Inspirational / Christian Fiction

| What you might want to flag | Why you shouldn't |
|---|---|
| Faith elements, prayer, scripture references | Core genre feature — readers seek this out |
| Characters turning to God during crisis | Expected and valued by the target reader |
| No physical intimacy beyond a kiss | That's the genre promise — readers count on it |
| Character's faith journey taking up significant page time | The spiritual arc is as important as the external plot |
| Happy, redemptive ending | Genre expectation — inspirational fiction delivers hope |
| Conservative worldview in characters' choices | Reflect the target reader's values — not a weakness |
| No profanity | Genre standard |

---

## Romance

| What you might want to flag | Why you shouldn't |
|---|---|
| Predictable HEA ending | That's the genre promise — readers WANT it |
| Instalove or fast attraction | Expected in some subgenres (holiday romance, shorter novellas) |
| "Tropes" like enemies-to-lovers or second chance | These are features, not clichés — readers buy the book for them |
| Characters thinking about each other constantly | That's romance doing its job |
| Minimal plot outside the relationship | In short contemporary romance, the relationship IS the plot |

### Cozy Romance specifically:
- Slower pacing — that's the warmth factor
- Community scenes and town events — that's the "cozy" part
- Low-conflict, warm tone — features, not weaknesses

### Dark Romance specifically:
- Morally gray or controlling heroes — genre expectation, not a character flaw
- Non-traditional consent dynamics (within stated content warnings) — genre convention
- Intensity and darkness — the reader bought this knowing what they were getting
- Villain love interests — a selling point in dark romance

### Historical Romance specifically:
- Slower pacing than contemporary — period-appropriate
- Formal dialogue — correct for the era
- Less female agency — historically accurate tension (while still delivering the romance)
- Long slow burn — expected

## Women's Fiction

| What you might want to flag | Why you shouldn't |
|---|---|
| Romance is secondary or unresolved | In women's fiction, romance is a subplot, not the A-plot |
| Ending isn't a full HEA | Women's fiction ends hopefully and realistically, not with guaranteed HEA |
| Story focused on inner transformation over plot events | That's the genre |
| Ensemble cast with multiple relationship threads | Women's fiction is built on relationship webs |
| Literary pacing | Women's fiction can be slower and more reflective than genre fiction |
| Ambiguous or bittersweet elements in the ending | Acceptable in women's fiction if the protagonist has grown |

---

## Cozy Mystery

| What you might want to flag | Why you shouldn't |
|---|---|
| Amateur sleuth (not a professional detective) | Defining feature of the genre |
| Low violence / off-page death | Cozy convention — graphic violence would break genre |
| Warm community and food scenes | The "cozy" in cozy mystery |
| Light tone despite a murder plot | That tonal balance is the whole point |
| Slower pacing than thriller | Correct for the genre |
| The sleuth solves the mystery (not police) | Expected |

---

## Thriller / Suspense

| What you might want to flag | Why you shouldn't |
|---|---|
| Short chapters ending on cliffhangers | Standard thriller pacing technique |
| Minimal romantic subplot or character development | Plot and tension are the priority |
| Morally complex protagonist | Thriller convention — the hero doesn't have to be likable |
| Bleak or ambiguous ending | Acceptable in thriller — not all books end happily |
| Extreme situations | Heightened stakes are a genre feature |

---

## Fantasy / Paranormal

| What you might want to flag | Why you shouldn't |
|---|---|
| Slower opening to establish the world | Worldbuilding requires setup — give it room |
| Magic/power rules that feel complex | Complexity can be a feature if internally consistent |
| Long descriptions of setting or lore | Expected in high fantasy — part of the experience |
| Non-human romantic leads | A selling point in paranormal romance |
| Dark themes and moral ambiguity | Fantasy embraces this |

---

## Literary / General Fiction

| What you might want to flag | Why you shouldn't |
|---|---|
| Slower pacing | Literary fiction prioritizes depth over speed |
| Unresolved or ambiguous ending | Literary convention — closure isn't always the goal |
| Limited plot events | Character and theme can carry the book |
| Dense, stylized prose | A feature of the genre for the right audience |
| Dark or uncomfortable subject matter | Literary fiction goes where other genres don't |
