# Genre-Specific Series Templates

Reference templates for common series structures. Each template provides a ready-to-customize skeleton that authors can adapt to their specific concept.

---

## 1. Contemporary Romance: 6-Book Small-Town Series

### Series Structure
- **Architecture:** Standalone Connected
- **Concept:** Six friends / siblings / coworkers in a small town each get their own love story. Shared setting, recurring cast, community as the connective tissue.
- **Theme:** Finding where you belong (each character is searching for home in a different way).
- **Heat Level:** Consistent across the series (author's choice), or gradually escalating if the audience builds tolerance.
- **Word Count:** 55,000-75,000 per book.

### Book-by-Book Skeleton

**Book 1: The Anchor Book**
- **Premise:** The most relatable character in the group faces a love story that introduces the reader to the town, the friend group, and the world. This is the front door to the series.
- **Arc:** Character who left town returns (or newcomer arrives). Falls for someone rooted in the community. Learns that the life they were chasing isn't the life they needed.
- **Trope suggestions:** Return to hometown, opposites attract, second chance.
- **Series function:** Establishes the town, introduces all six future protagonists as side characters, sets the tone. Every future protagonist should have at least one memorable scene.
- **Resolution:** Full HEA. The couple is together. No cliffhanger.
- **Seeds planted:** Two side characters share a charged moment that goes nowhere — yet. One character is mentioned as having a complicated past. The town's annual event is referenced (will recur in every book).

**Book 2: The Fan Favorite**
- **Premise:** The side character readers loved most in Book 1 gets their story. Often the funny one, the protective best friend, or the grumpy loner.
- **Arc:** Character who seems like they have it all together is actually struggling. Their love interest sees through the facade. Vulnerability is the journey.
- **Trope suggestions:** Grumpy/sunshine, forced proximity, fake dating.
- **Series function:** Deepens the friend group dynamics. Book 1 couple makes a cameo (happy, established, not the focus). Introduces a community conflict that will simmer for several books.
- **Resolution:** Full HEA. The community conflict is acknowledged but not resolved.
- **Seeds planted:** A character from outside the friend group catches someone's eye. A family secret is hinted at.

**Book 3: The Emotional Gut Punch**
- **Premise:** The character with the most emotional baggage gets their story. This is the book that makes readers cry.
- **Arc:** Overcoming past trauma to let love in. The love interest is patient and safe. The character learns they deserve happiness.
- **Trope suggestions:** Friends to lovers (slow burn), hurt/comfort, single parent.
- **Series function:** The emotional center of the series. Raises the stakes for what love costs in this world. The community rallies — showing the town as a character in itself.
- **Resolution:** Full HEA. The most cathartic of the series.
- **Seeds planted:** A misunderstanding between two other characters creates tension that will fuel a future book. A new business/event/tradition is established that changes the town slightly.

**Book 4: The Wildcard**
- **Premise:** The unexpected pairing. Two characters readers didn't see coming together. Or the character nobody thought would get a love story.
- **Arc:** Challenges assumptions — both the characters' and the readers'. Love shows up where you least expect it.
- **Trope suggestions:** Enemies to lovers, age gap, forbidden (within the friend group dynamics).
- **Series function:** Shakes up the group dynamic. Creates a ripple that forces other characters to confront their own feelings. The community conflict from Book 2 escalates.
- **Resolution:** Full HEA, but it changes the friend group's equilibrium.
- **Seeds planted:** The penultimate couple is now circling each other with obvious tension. A character makes a decision that will set up the final book.

**Book 5: The Slow Burn Payoff**
- **Premise:** The couple that has been building tension since Book 1 or 2 finally gets their story. Readers have been waiting for this one.
- **Arc:** Everything that kept them apart gets dismantled. The longest buildup gets the most satisfying payoff.
- **Trope suggestions:** Slow burn (obviously), will-they-won't-they, one bed (finally).
- **Series function:** Second-to-last book. The community conflict comes to a head. The friend group faces a challenge that requires them to work together.
- **Resolution:** Full HEA. The community conflict resolves or nearly resolves.
- **Seeds planted:** The final protagonist is clearly the last one standing. Set up their love interest in a way that makes readers desperate for Book 6.

**Book 6: The Grand Finale**
- **Premise:** The last character gets their love story. Often the one who's been everyone else's rock, the organizer, the one who put everyone else first.
- **Arc:** Learning to prioritize their own happiness. Accepting help and love after being the strong one for everyone else.
- **Trope suggestions:** Surprise romance, brother's best friend / best friend's sibling, the one who was always there.
- **Series function:** Brings the whole series together. Cameos from every couple. A group event (wedding, festival, reunion) that celebrates the community. Leaves the reader with the feeling that this town is a real place full of real people.
- **Resolution:** Full HEA. Series-level satisfaction: everyone is happy, the town is thriving, the friend group is whole.

### Character Rotation Plan

| Character | Bk 1 | Bk 2 | Bk 3 | Bk 4 | Bk 5 | Bk 6 |
|-----------|-------|-------|-------|-------|-------|-------|
| Protagonist A | **LEAD** | Cameo | Cameo | Minor | Cameo | Cameo |
| Protagonist B | Side | **LEAD** | Minor | Cameo | Cameo | Cameo |
| Protagonist C | Side | Side | **LEAD** | Cameo | Minor | Cameo |
| Protagonist D | Mentioned | Side | Side | **LEAD** | Cameo | Cameo |
| Protagonist E | Side | Side | Minor | Side | **LEAD** | Cameo |
| Protagonist F | Side | Side | Side | Side | Side | **LEAD** |

**Rule:** Once a character has had their spotlight book, they appear in subsequent books only in cameos or minor roles. They are happy. Do not create drama for established couples.

### Tension Mapping

```

Book 2: |||||||--- (7/10) — Deeper. Funnier or sexier. "This series is good."
Book 3: |||||||||-- (9/10) — Emotional peak. "I'm sobbing." The book readers recommend.
Book 4: |||||||--- (7/10) — Surprising. Energizing. "I didn't expect that."

```

### Release Strategy
- **Write Books 1-3 before publishing Book 1.** Rapid release the first three at 3-4 week intervals.
- **Books 4-6:** Release every 6-8 weeks after the initial burst.
- **Total series timeline:** 6-9 months from first to last release.
- **Book 1 pricing:** $0.99 or permafree for the first 30 days to hook readers into the series.
- **Box set:** Release a Books 1-3 bundle after Book 4 launches. Full series box set after Book 6.
- **Pre-orders:** Always have the next book available for pre-order. Back matter of every book links to the next.

## 2. Dark Fantasy / Romantasy: 4-Book Sequential Series

### Series Structure
- **Architecture:** Sequential (must read in order)
- **Concept:** One protagonist's journey through a dark, magical world. A central romance that builds across all four books alongside an epic-scale conflict. Fated mates, morally gray love interest, a heroine who comes into her power.
- **Theme:** Power and its cost — who you become when you stop being afraid.
- **Heat Level:** Escalating. Book 1: tension and near-misses. Book 2: first encounter. Book 3-4: full heat.
- **Word Count:** 85,000-110,000 per book.

### Book-by-Book Skeleton

**Book 1: The Awakening**
- **Premise:** The protagonist discovers they are not who they thought they were. Thrust into a world of magic, politics, and danger. Meets the love interest under adversarial circumstances.
- **Arc:** From ordinary/powerless to aware. The character doesn't master their power — they survive learning it exists. The romance is antagonistic: distrust, forced alliance, reluctant attraction.
- **Series function:** World-building foundation. Establish the magic system, the political landscape, the threat. End with the protagonist choosing to stay in this world rather than flee.
- **Resolved:** The immediate threat (the inciting incident crisis). The protagonist's denial about who they are.
- **Carries forward:** The larger political/magical conflict. The romance (unresolved tension). A betrayal or reveal that reframes everything.
- **Emotional trajectory:** Overwhelmed but determined. The reader should feel the protagonist's world has cracked open.

**Book 2: The Descent**
- **Premise:** The protagonist trains, allies shift, and the true scope of the conflict becomes clear. The romance deepens through vulnerability — both characters reveal their damage. A major loss forces the protagonist to fight harder.
- **Arc:** From reactive to proactive. The character makes choices that have consequences. The romance crosses a line — they act on the attraction, but it complicates everything.
- **Series function:** Deepen the world. Introduce the real antagonist (or reveal that the perceived antagonist is a pawn). Expand the magic system. The protagonist gains power but loses something precious.
- **Resolved:** A significant battle or confrontation. A secondary character's arc. The "will they or won't they" of the romance (they will — but at a cost).
- **Carries forward:** The main conflict escalates. A new threat emerges. The love interest's secret or divided loyalty.
- **Emotional trajectory:** Exhilarated and heartbroken. The reader should feel the stakes are real.

**Book 3: The Reckoning**
- **Premise:** Everything falls apart. Alliances shatter. The protagonist's power becomes a double-edged sword. The romance faces its crisis — a separation, a betrayal, a sacrifice. The darkest book in the series.
- **Arc:** From confident to broken to rebuilt. This is the crucible. The character must choose between power and connection, duty and desire. They lose before they can win.
- **Series function:** The point of no return. Strip away everything the protagonist has built. Force the hardest choice. End on a devastating but hopeful note — they know what they have to do, even if it might destroy them.
- **Resolved:** The secondary villain or the political subplot. A long-running betrayal is exposed.
- **Carries forward:** The main conflict reaches its peak. The romance is in shambles but not dead. The protagonist has a plan — or a terrible, beautiful, desperate idea.
- **Emotional trajectory:** Devastated but not hopeless. The reader should feel that everything hinges on Book 4.

**Book 4: The Ascension**
- **Premise:** The final confrontation. The protagonist uses everything they've learned — power, allies, sacrifice, love — to face the ultimate threat. The romance resolves: they fight their way back to each other. The world is remade.
- **Arc:** From broken to transcendent. Not just returning to who they were — becoming who they were always meant to be. The romance is the anchor, not the distraction.
- **Series function:** Pay off every thread. Every planted seed blooms. Every sacrifice means something. The ending must be proportional to the investment. If the reader spent four books in this world, the climax should be seismic.
- **Resolved:** Everything. The main conflict. The romance. The political landscape. The protagonist's identity. Leave no major thread hanging.
- **Carries forward:** Nothing plot-critical. But leave the world alive — readers should feel the world continues beyond the last page. (This also leaves room for spinoffs.)
- **Emotional trajectory:** Catharsis. Relief. Triumph. The reader should feel like they survived something alongside the protagonist.

### Character Rotation Plan

| Character | Bk 1 | Bk 2 | Bk 3 | Bk 4 |
|-----------|-------|-------|-------|-------|
| Protagonist | **POV** | **POV** | **POV** | **POV** |
| Love Interest | Antagonist/Ally | Ally/Lover | Separated/Crisis | Reunited/Partner |
| Mentor Figure | Guide | Tested loyalty | Absent or dead | Legacy felt |
| Best Friend/Ally | Comic relief/heart | Comes into own | Makes sacrifice | Rewarded or mourned |
| Antagonist | Shadowed/hinted | Revealed | Ascendant | Confronted and resolved |
| Secondary Romance Pair | Introduced | Tension builds | Crisis mirrors main | Resolution mirrors main |

### Tension Mapping

```
Book 1: |||||||--- (7/10) — Discovery and danger. High for a first book.
Book 2: ||||||||-- (8/10) — Escalation. Deeper, darker, sexier.
Book 3: |||||||||| (10/10) — The gut punch. Maximum pain. Everything breaks.
Book 4: ||||||||||- (9.5/10) — Climax and resolution. Tension peaks then releases into catharsis.
```

### Release Strategy
- **Write all four books before publishing Book 1.** Sequential series live or die on momentum and consistency.
- **Rapid release at 4-6 week intervals.** Romantasy readers consume fast.
- **Total series timeline:** 4-6 months from first to last release.
- **Cover design:** Unified aesthetic across all four. Commissioned art or a consistent design template. The covers should look like a set.
- **Book 1 pricing:** $2.99-4.99 launch. Consider a 99-cent sale after Book 3 launches to pull in new readers before the finale.
- **Marketing:** Heavy BookTok/Bookstagram push. Romantasy is a visual-discovery genre. Aesthetic boards, fan-cast posts, quote graphics.
- **Box set:** Full series box set 3-6 months after Book 4. Price at $9.99-14.99.

## 3. Cozy Mystery: 8-Book Episodic Series

### Series Structure
- **Architecture:** Standalone Connected (each book is one case, shared protagonist and world)
- **Concept:** An amateur sleuth in a charming setting solves murders that keep happening in their orbit. The setting is as important as the mystery — a bakery, a bookshop, a B&B, a knitting circle. Light tone, no graphic violence, a community of quirky regulars.
- **Theme:** Community resilience — bad things happen, but good people look out for each other.
- **Heat Level:** Clean to sweet. Romance is a subplot at most.
- **Word Count:** 55,000-70,000 per book.

### Book-by-Book Skeleton

**Book 1: The Introduction**
- **Premise:** The sleuth arrives in (or returns to) the setting. A murder disrupts the charming world. The sleuth gets pulled in, solves the case, and establishes themselves in the community.
- **Arc:** Outsider to insider. Or: person running from their old life discovers they're exactly where they need to be.
- **Case:** A body at a community event. A victim everyone had a reason to dislike. The solution reveals something about the town's hidden dynamics.
- **Series seeds:** Introduce the full recurring cast (best friend, love interest, rival, quirky neighbor, suspicious local). Establish the setting in rich, cozy detail. Introduce the sleuth's business/passion that anchors them to the location.

**Book 2: The Establishment**
- **Premise:** A case that hits closer to home — the victim is someone the sleuth knows (not well, but enough to feel it). The community is shaken. The sleuth's reputation as a solver grows.
- **Arc:** The sleuth commits to the community. They stop treating this as temporary. Romantic subplot simmers.
- **Case:** More complex than Book 1. A second suspect who seems obviously guilty but isn't. A twist that requires local knowledge to solve.
- **Series seeds:** A recurring antagonist figure (not a killer — a rival, a skeptical cop, a nosy reporter) who will provide friction across the series.

**Book 3: The Complication**
- **Premise:** The sleuth is now known as someone who solves things. Someone comes to them for help. The case involves the sleuth's own business or passion (the bakery is the crime scene, the bookshop holds the clue).
- **Arc:** Personal stakes. What the sleuth loves is threatened. They fight for their place in the community.
- **Case:** The most personal case yet. The solution requires the sleuth to confront something about themselves.

**Book 4: The Deepening**
- **Premise:** A case from the past resurfaces. Cold case or a new murder linked to old events. The town's history is the mystery.
- **Arc:** The sleuth learns the community isn't as simple as it seemed. Secrets lurk. The romantic subplot progresses significantly.
- **Case:** Multi-layered. Past and present intertwined. Older community members become central. The town's origin story or a founding family's secret.

**Book 5: The Outsider**
- **Premise:** An outsider disrupts the community. A developer, a celebrity, a long-lost relative, a journalist. The murder is connected to what they brought with them.
- **Arc:** The community bands together. The sleuth defends their town. The outsider forces everyone to examine what they value.
- **Case:** Higher stakes — the outsider's world brings more dangerous people and motives. The solution requires the sleuth to think beyond their usual patterns.

**Book 6: The Personal Crisis**
- **Premise:** The sleuth's personal life becomes the story. A family member visits and brings trouble. Or the romantic relationship hits a crisis point. The case intertwines with the personal drama.
- **Arc:** The sleuth as a person, not just a solver. Vulnerability. Growth. The community supports them for a change.
- **Case:** The most emotionally complex case. The killer's motive is sympathetic. The solution isn't clean.

**Book 7: The High Stakes**
- **Premise:** The biggest case yet. Multiple victims, or a threat that endangers the whole community. The sleuth's reputation and relationships are all on the line.
- **Arc:** Everything the sleuth has built is tested. Their skills, their relationships, their commitment. This is the series climax even though there's one more book.
- **Case:** The most complex, highest-stakes mystery. A satisfying, surprising solution that uses everything the reader has learned about the community.

**Book 8: The Resolution**
- **Premise:** A case that brings the series full circle. Connected to Book 1's events, or to the sleuth's reason for coming to the community in the first place. Lower stakes than Book 7 — this is a victory lap.
- **Arc:** The sleuth is fully home. Romantic resolution (if applicable). The community is the sleuth's family. The case is solved with the help of everyone the sleuth has befriended along the way.
- **Case:** A satisfying puzzle that rewards long-time readers with callbacks and connections. The solution reveals one last secret about the community that reframes everything in a warm light.

### Character Rotation Plan

| Character | Role | Presence |
|-----------|------|----------|
| Sleuth | Protagonist, every book | Always POV, always central |
| Best Friend | Sidekick/sounding board | Every book, varying involvement |
| Love Interest | Slow-burn romantic subplot | Every book, progressing from tension to resolution |
| Rival/Antagonist | Friction source | Most books, sometimes ally |
| Law Enforcement Contact | Official channel | Every book, growing respect for sleuth |
| Quirky Regular #1 | Comic relief, occasional witness | Every book, brief appearances |
| Quirky Regular #2 | Wisdom dispenser, red herring source | Every book, brief appearances |
| Victim (rotating) | New each book | Introduced and killed per book |
| Suspects (rotating) | 3-5 per book, new each time | Per book, though some may recur as cleared suspects |

### Tension Mapping

```
Book 1: ||||||----- (6/10) — Light, fun, establishing. The gateway drug.
Book 2: ||||||----- (6/10) — Steady. Slightly more personal stakes.
Book 3: |||||||---- (7/10) — Personal threat. Sleuth's world is at risk.
Book 4: |||||||---- (7/10) — Atmospheric. The town's dark past.
Book 5: |||||||---- (7/10) — External threat. Community vs. outsider.
Book 6: ||||||||--- (8/10) — Emotional peak. The sleuth is vulnerable.
Book 7: |||||||||-- (9/10) — Series climax. Maximum danger.
Book 8: ||||||---- (6/10) — Warm resolution. Full circle. Cozy.
```

### Release Strategy
- **Write Books 1-3 before publishing.** Cozy readers read fast and expect consistency.
- **Release every 3-4 months.** Cozy readers are voracious but patient if the schedule is predictable.
- **Total series timeline:** 18-24 months.
- **Pricing:** Book 1 at $0.99 or permafree. Remaining books at $3.99-4.99.
- **Kindle Unlimited:** Almost mandatory for cozy mystery. This is a KU-heavy genre.
- **Box sets:** Books 1-3 bundle after Book 4 launches. Books 4-6 bundle after Book 7. Full series set after Book 8.
- **Seasonal timing:** If the setting has seasonal elements, align releases with seasons (holiday cozy in November, summer cozy in June).

## 4. Psychological Thriller: 3-Book Trilogy

### Series Structure
- **Architecture:** Sequential with possible POV shifts
- **Concept:** A single, escalating threat explored from different angles across three books. Each book reveals a new layer of the truth. The reader thinks they understand the story until the next book reframes everything.
- **Theme:** Trust and perception — nothing is what it seems, and everyone is complicit.
- **Heat Level:** N/A or minimal. Tension is the engine, not romance.
- **Word Count:** 75,000-90,000 per book.

### Book-by-Book Skeleton

**Book 1: The Surface**
- **Premise:** An apparently isolated incident — a disappearance, a death, a betrayal — in a contained setting (a marriage, a neighborhood, a workplace, a family). The protagonist investigates or is caught in the fallout. The truth seems clear by the end, but something doesn't sit right.
- **Arc:** The protagonist trusts their perception. They solve the surface-level mystery. But the solution they arrive at is incomplete. They don't know it yet. The reader might suspect, but shouldn't be certain.
- **Resolution:** The immediate case is "solved." The protagonist's life returns to a new normal. But the final chapter or epilogue introduces a detail that makes the reader question everything.
- **Carries forward:** A character who seemed peripheral is more important than they appeared. A piece of evidence that was dismissed. The protagonist's own reliability.

**Book 2: The Fracture**
- **Premise:** The "solved" case from Book 1 unravels. A new event — or a new perspective — reveals that the truth was deeper and darker than anyone realized. The protagonist is pulled back in, or a new protagonist discovers what the first one missed.
- **Arc:** The comfortable narrative shatters. Characters the reader trusted become suspects. The protagonist (new or returning) must question everything — including themselves. This is the darkest book.
- **Resolution:** The true scope of the conspiracy/threat/lie is revealed. The antagonist's identity shifts — or multiplies. The protagonist faces a terrible choice.
- **Carries forward:** The choice. The fallout. The reader now has all the pieces but hasn't assembled them yet.

**Book 3: The Truth**
- **Premise:** The final confrontation. All perspectives converge. The full truth is revealed, and it's worse — and more human — than expected. The protagonist must act on what they know, even though every option has a cost.
- **Arc:** From fractured understanding to complete clarity. The protagonist assembles the full picture and makes their choice. The ending is satisfying but morally complex — not everything can be made right.
- **Resolution:** Everything. Every planted detail from Books 1 and 2 pays off. The reader should want to immediately re-read the series to see what they missed. The ending should be inevitable in retrospect but surprising in the moment.

### Character Rotation Plan

| Character | Bk 1 | Bk 2 | Bk 3 |
|-----------|-------|-------|-------|
| Protagonist A | **POV** — reliable narrator (maybe) | Supporting or absent | Returns with new understanding |
| Protagonist B (if applicable) | Side character or unseen | **POV** — reveals what A missed | Converges with A |
| The Antagonist | Present but disguised | Exposed or reframed | Confronted |
| The Victim | Central | Recontextualized | Fully understood |
| The Witness | Peripheral | Key | Pivotal |

**Option 1:** Same protagonist across all three books, with their perception and reliability shifting.
**Option 2:** Different POV each book (e.g., the wife, the detective, the killer). Each perspective reveals what the others couldn't see.
**Option 3:** Same protagonist but alternating timelines — present, past, truth.

### Tension Mapping

```
Book 1: ||||||||--- (8/10) — Taut. Gripping. Feels like a complete thriller. But the ground is unstable.
Book 2: |||||||||| (10/10) — Everything breaks. The reader doesn't know who to trust. Peak dread.
Book 3: ||||||||||- (9.5/10) — Resolution through revelation. Tension shifts from dread to moral complexity.
```

### Release Strategy
- **Write all three before publishing.** A trilogy lives or dies on consistency and planned reveals. You cannot pants a psychological thriller trilogy.
- **Release 6-8 weeks apart.** Fast enough to maintain the "what really happened?" urgency.
- **Total series timeline:** 3-4 months.
- **Book 1 pricing:** $3.99-4.99. Thriller readers pay full price for a gripping Book 1.
- **Marketing:** "If you liked [comp], you'll love this." Thriller readers are comp-driven. Lead with the hook, not the series structure.
- **Cover design:** Unified. Same typography, color palette, and design language. The covers should look like they belong together on a shelf.
- **Box set:** Full trilogy box set 2-3 months after Book 3. Price at $7.99-9.99.
- **Audio:** Thriller is massive in audio. Plan for audiobook production simultaneously. Same narrator across all three books is critical.

## 5. Epic Fantasy: 5-Book Saga

### Series Structure
- **Architecture:** Sequential (must read in order)
- **Concept:** A world-spanning conflict told across five books with escalating scope. Multiple POV characters whose stories interweave. A magic system that deepens with each book. Political intrigue, war, personal sacrifice, and a final confrontation that reshapes the world.
- **Theme:** The price of saving a world — what is lost and what endures.
- **Heat Level:** Varies by author. The romance is a subplot, not the engine.
- **Word Count:** 90,000-130,000 per book.

### Book-by-Book Skeleton

**Book 1: The Shattering**
- **Premise:** The status quo breaks. An event disrupts the world order and forces the protagonist(s) into motion. The scope is personal — one person's life changes — but the implications are world-sized. The magic system is introduced through experience, not exposition.
- **Arc:** From ordinary to called. The protagonist doesn't choose the adventure — it chooses them. By the end, they've committed to the path, but they don't understand the true scope of what they're facing.
- **World-building focus:** The home region. Culture, daily life, local politics, the magic system's basics. Show the world worth saving before you threaten it.
- **Resolution:** The immediate crisis is resolved or survived. The protagonist leaves home (literally or metaphorically). A mentor figure provides direction.
- **Carries forward:** The larger threat is named but not understood. An artifact, a prophecy, a political shift — something that will drive the next four books. A secondary character begins their own parallel arc.

**Book 2: The Expansion**
- **Premise:** The world gets bigger. New regions, new cultures, new factions. The protagonist(s) travel, train, or infiltrate. Alliances form. The magic system reveals deeper layers. A secondary POV character becomes co-protagonist.
- **Arc:** From naive to tested. The protagonist makes mistakes with real consequences. The secondary protagonist faces a moral dilemma that mirrors the main one from a different angle. A key relationship deepens (romantic, mentor-student, or sworn-enemy-turned-ally).
- **World-building focus:** The wider world. Other nations, other magic traditions, other perspectives on the conflict. The reader's understanding of the world doubles.
- **Resolution:** A significant battle or political turning point. The protagonist levels up but pays a price. A secondary character betrays or is betrayed.
- **Carries forward:** The true antagonist's plan becomes clearer. The protagonist's power grows but so does the cost. Factions are aligning for war.

**Book 3: The Crucible (Midpoint)**
- **Premise:** The center of the series. The protagonist faces the consequences of their choices so far. A major loss — a death, a defeat, a betrayal — changes the trajectory. The magic system is pushed to its limits and something breaks or transforms.
- **Arc:** From tested to broken to rebuilt. This is the empire-strikes-back book. The protagonist fails, or wins at too high a cost, or discovers the truth about their cause/power/identity. Everything they believed is challenged.
- **World-building focus:** The underground. Secrets, ancient history, forbidden magic, hidden factions. The world's depths, not its breadth.
- **Resolution:** The old plan is destroyed. A new path emerges from the wreckage. A character thought lost returns, or a character thought safe is lost. The midpoint pivot changes everything.
- **Carries forward:** The protagonist knows what they must do but not if they can survive it. The political landscape has shifted — old allies are enemies, old enemies might be allies. The magic system has a new dimension that changes the calculus.

**Book 4: The Convergence**
- **Premise:** All POV characters' stories begin merging. The final conflict takes shape. Armies gather, alliances are tested, the antagonist makes their move. The protagonist makes sacrifices to position for the endgame.
- **Arc:** From rebuilt to resolute. The protagonist has clarity but not certainty. They gather their allies, confront the costs, and prepare for the final confrontation. Secondary characters complete their individual arcs or make their final choices.
- **World-building focus:** The stakes. What the world will look like if the protagonist fails. What it will cost even if they win. The history and mythology pay off — ancient connections become clear.
- **Resolution:** The penultimate confrontation. A significant victory at a significant cost. The stage is set for the finale. Every character is in position.
- **Carries forward:** One final secret, one final complication, one final sacrifice required. The reader should be unable to stop before Book 5.

**Book 5: The Reforging**
- **Premise:** The final battle for the world. Every thread converges. Every planted seed blooms. Every sacrifice is honored. The protagonist faces the ultimate test — not just of power, but of character. The magic system is used in a way that redefines it.
- **Arc:** From resolute to transcendent. The protagonist doesn't just win — they transform the terms of victory. The theme (the price of saving a world) is answered. Some things are lost forever. Some things endure.
- **World-building focus:** The aftermath. What the world becomes. The reader should see the future they invested five books in.
- **Resolution:** Everything. The main conflict. Every character's arc. The romantic subplot. The political landscape. The magic system's ultimate implication. The world is changed, and the reader understands how and why.

### Character Rotation Plan

| Character | Bk 1 | Bk 2 | Bk 3 | Bk 4 | Bk 5 |
|-----------|-------|-------|-------|-------|-------|
| Primary Protagonist | **POV** - called | **POV** - tested | **POV** - broken | **POV** - resolute | **POV** - transformed |
| Secondary Protagonist | Introduced | **POV added** | POV - dilemma | POV - convergence | POV - resolved |
| Antagonist | Shadowed | Agents act | Personally involved | Makes move | Confronted |
| Mentor | Active guide | Tested loyalty | Lost or absent | Legacy | Honored |
| Ally-turned-complex | Friend | Friend with doubts | Betrayer or betrayed | Reckoning | Resolved (death or redemption) |
| Romantic Interest | Met | Deepened | Separated by crisis | Reunited with tension | Resolved |

**POV management:** Start with 1-2 POVs in Book 1. Expand to 3-4 by Book 3. Contract back to 2-3 in Book 5. The reader should feel the world expanding and then focusing.

### Tension Mapping

```
Book 1: ||||||---- (6/10) — Wonder and discovery. Personal stakes. The world is beautiful and threatened.
Book 2: ||||||||-- (8/10) — Expansion and consequence. The world is bigger and more dangerous.
Book 3: |||||||||| (10/10) — The gut punch. Maximum loss. Everything changes.
Book 4: |||||||||-- (9/10) — Convergence and sacrifice. Relentless momentum.
Book 5: ||||||||||- (9.5/10) — Climax and catharsis. The payoff of five books.
```

### Release Strategy
- **Write at least Books 1-3 before publishing.** Epic fantasy readers demand consistency. Plot holes in a 5-book fantasy saga are unforgivable.
- **Release every 6-9 months.** Fantasy readers will wait for quality, but don't make them wait more than a year.
- **Total series timeline:** 2-3 years.
- **Pricing:** $4.99-5.99 per book. Fantasy readers accept higher price points for longer books.
- **Maps and appendices:** Include a map in Book 1. Add a glossary by Book 2. Provide a character list by Book 3. These are expected in epic fantasy.
- **Cover design:** Iconic. Unified. Art that readers want as posters. Fantasy covers sell books.
- **Hardcover/special editions:** Plan for special editions of the full series after completion. Fantasy readers are collectors.
- **Audio:** Essential. Fantasy audiobooks need a skilled narrator who can handle multiple characters, accents, and invented words. Lock in the narrator for all five books.
- **Box set:** Digital box set after completion at $14.99-19.99. Physical box set for collectors.

## 6. Paranormal Romance: 5-Book Clan/Pack/Coven Series

### Series Structure
- **Architecture:** Hybrid (standalone romances that build toward a series-level threat)
- **Concept:** A group of connected paranormal beings (a wolf pack, a vampire clan, a witch coven, a fae court) each gets their own love story while a larger supernatural threat builds across the series. Each book is a complete romance with an HEA, but the series-level antagonist grows more dangerous until the final book where the couple's romance AND the supernatural threat climax together.
- **Theme:** Found family and belonging — each character finds their mate AND their place in the group.
- **Heat Level:** High. Paranormal romance readers expect heat. The paranormal element often amplifies intimacy (mate bonds, psychic connections, heightened senses).
- **Word Count:** 70,000-90,000 per book.

### Book-by-Book Skeleton

**Book 1: The Alpha/Leader**
- **Premise:** The pack/clan/coven leader — the most powerful, most controlled, most closed-off member of the group — meets their match. The love interest is an outsider who disrupts the established order. The supernatural threat is introduced as a distant storm.
- **Arc:** The leader learns that strength isn't the same as control. The love interest forces them to be vulnerable. The mating bond (or equivalent) complicates everything — it's undeniable but the leader resists it.
- **Romance arc:** Enemies/strangers to reluctant allies to surrendering to the bond. The love interest proves their worth to the group on their own terms, not by submitting to the hierarchy.
- **Series threat:** Introduced in the background. A missing member, a strange occurrence, a warning from another clan. Enough to create unease but not enough to overshadow the romance.
- **Resolution:** Full HEA. The leader and their mate are bonded. The love interest is accepted by the group. The threat is acknowledged but not confronted.
- **Seeds planted:** Introduce all five future protagonists. At least two of them should have a moment that makes readers curious. The series threat's nature is hinted at.

**Book 2: The Rebel**
- **Premise:** The most defiant, least obedient member of the group gets their story. Their love interest is someone who represents everything the group stands for — or everything the rebel rejected. The supernatural threat makes its first move.
- **Arc:** The rebel learns that belonging isn't the same as conforming. They find their own way to be part of the group without losing who they are. Their love interest challenges their assumptions about duty, loyalty, and freedom.
- **Romance arc:** Opposites attract. Explosive chemistry from the start, emotional intimacy takes longer. A moment where the rebel must choose between independence and their mate — and discovers it's a false choice.
- **Series threat:** Escalates. An attack, a disappearance, a power surge. The group responds. The rebel's unique abilities or perspective become relevant to fighting the threat.
- **Resolution:** Full HEA. The rebel is committed — to their mate and to the group, in their own way. The threat is fought but not defeated.
- **Seeds planted:** The next protagonist is revealed to have a secret that connects to the series threat. A power dynamic within the group shifts.

**Book 3: The Wounded One**
- **Premise:** The member of the group carrying the most damage — trauma, loss, a dark past, a curse — gets their story. Their love interest is a healer, a protector, or someone who sees past the damage to the person underneath. The supernatural threat becomes personal.
- **Arc:** Healing is not linear. The character pushes their love interest away, self-destructs, and must choose to let someone in. The group rallies around them but can't do the work for them.
- **Romance arc:** Hurt/comfort at its deepest. Slow burn. The physical intimacy is tied to emotional safety — when they finally come together, it means everything. The mate bond (if applicable) is a source of terror before it's a source of comfort.
- **Series threat:** The threat targets the wounded character specifically, or exploits their vulnerability. This makes the threat personal and raises the stakes for the whole group.
- **Resolution:** Full HEA. The character is not "fixed" but is choosing healing with their mate's support. The threat has been fought to a standstill. The group knows the final confrontation is coming.
- **Seeds planted:** The antagonist's true plan is partially revealed. The remaining two protagonists' tension with each other reaches a breaking point.

**Book 4: The Hidden One**
- **Premise:** The member with the secret — hidden power, hidden identity, hidden connection to the enemy — gets their story. Their love interest is someone who makes keeping the secret impossible. The supernatural threat's true nature is revealed.
- **Arc:** The character must choose between safety (keeping the secret) and authenticity (revealing who they really are). The secret, when revealed, changes the group's understanding of the threat and of themselves.
- **Romance arc:** Built on honesty. The love interest discovers the secret and must decide if they can accept it. A separation when the truth comes out, then a reunion built on complete transparency. The most emotionally complex romance in the series.
- **Series threat:** The big reveal. The antagonist's plan, identity, or connection to the group becomes clear. The hidden character's secret is the key to defeating the threat — or the reason the threat exists.
- **Resolution:** Full HEA. The secret is out, accepted, and integrated. The group is fully unified and preparing for the final battle. The antagonist has made their last move.
- **Seeds planted:** The final protagonist is positioned for the climactic book. Their love interest is identified and the stakes between them are sky-high.

**Book 5: The Sacrifice / The Unifier**
- **Premise:** The final couple's romance happens against the backdrop of the ultimate confrontation with the series threat. This character is the one who holds the group together — the heart, the conscience, the bridge between factions. Their love interest is entangled with the threat in a way that makes the romance itself part of the solution.
- **Arc:** The character must balance saving the group with saving their relationship. The mate bond is tested to its absolute limit. The resolution requires sacrifice — but the series should define what "sacrifice" means in a way that doesn't destroy the HEA.
- **Romance arc:** The most intense romance in the series. The highest stakes, the most at risk, the most to lose. Every previous couple's love story built to this — the belief that love is worth fighting for, demonstrated at the highest level.
- **Series threat:** Climax and resolution. The full group — all five couples — contribute to the final battle. Every power, every bond, every sacrifice matters. The threat is defeated in a way that could only work because of everything that came before.
- **Resolution:** Full HEA for the final couple. Full series resolution. The clan/pack/coven is stronger than ever. Every couple gets a moment. The world is safe. The family is complete.

### Character Rotation Plan

| Character | Bk 1 | Bk 2 | Bk 3 | Bk 4 | Bk 5 |
|-----------|-------|-------|-------|-------|-------|
| The Alpha/Leader | **LEAD** | Mentor role | Support | Support | Battle leader |
| The Rebel | Side (memorable) | **LEAD** | Ally | Ally | Soldier |
| The Wounded One | Side (hints of pain) | Side (withdrawn) | **LEAD** | Support | Healed strength |
| The Hidden One | Side (mysterious) | Side (suspicious) | Side (cracking) | **LEAD** | Key to the fight |
| The Heart/Unifier | Side (warmth) | Side (holding things together) | Side (strained) | Side (positioned) | **LEAD** |
| Antagonist | Hinted | First move | Personal attack | Revealed | Confronted |

**Love interest appearances:** Each love interest should appear (or be mentioned) at least one book before their spotlight book. A brief encounter, a name dropped, a charged moment. Build anticipation.

### Tension Mapping

```
Book 1: ||||||---- (6/10) — Steamy and exciting. The threat is distant thunder.
Book 2: |||||||--- (7/10) — Hotter. The threat moves closer. The group tightens.
Book 3: ||||||||-- (8/10) — Emotional devastation. The threat gets personal.
Book 4: |||||||||-- (9/10) — Revelation. Everything changes. The stakes are clear.
```

### Release Strategy
- **Write Books 1-3 before publishing.** PNR readers consume fast and expect momentum.
- **Release every 4-6 weeks for the first three books.** Rapid release captures the binge-reader audience.
- **Books 4-5:** Release every 6-8 weeks. The audience is locked in by now.
- **Total series timeline:** 6-9 months.
- **Pricing:** Book 1 at $2.99-3.99. Remaining books at $4.99.
- **Kindle Unlimited:** Strongly recommended. PNR is a KU-dominant genre.
- **Cover design:** Cohesive aesthetic. Same font treatment, same style. Character-forward covers work well in PNR — illustrated or photo-manipulated.
- **Marketing:** Lead with the paranormal hook AND the romance. "Fated mates wolf shifter romance" or "Enemies-to-lovers vampire romance." PNR readers search by trope AND subgenre. Keywords and categories matter enormously.
- **Box set:** Books 1-3 bundle after Book 4. Full series box after Book 5. PNR box sets sell exceptionally well at $9.99.
- **Bonus content:** Consider a bonus novella or epilogue collection after the series wraps — a "where are they now" with all five couples. PNR readers are invested in the group and want to see them all together.

## Using These Templates

These are skeletons, not prescriptions. Every series will diverge from its template once the author's specific characters, world, and voice take over. The value of the template is in the structural thinking:

- **Tension mapping** prevents sagging middles and rushed endings.
- **Character rotation** ensures every future protagonist is set up before their book.
- **Release strategy** keeps the business plan aligned with the creative plan.
- **Series architecture** decisions made early prevent structural problems that are expensive to fix later.

Start with the template closest to your series. Customize aggressively. Then use the Series Architect skill to build out the full bible with your specific details.
