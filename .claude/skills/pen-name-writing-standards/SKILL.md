# Pen Name Writing Standards

This skill carries Sylvia's standing F.B. Publishing fiction instructions so the user does not need to upload the Word document in every writing chat.

## Always Load

Read `references/pen-name-instructions-june2026.md` before drafting, revising, polishing, critiquing, outlining, beta reading, editing, or proofreading fiction for Sylvia or any of her pen names.

## Relationship To Other Skills

- Use this skill as the governing project/pen-name standard.
- Use `ai-tell-eliminator` at the same time for fiction prose whenever it is available.
- Use genre skills, outline skills, author skills, editor skills, `author-voice-lock`, `natural-dialogue`, `outline-critique`, `chapter-by-chapter-edit`, `manuscript-editing`, or `full-stack-manuscript-polish` as needed, but do not let them override these standing standards.
- The user's newest instruction in the current conversation overrides this reference only when it is explicit.

## Non-Negotiable Defaults

- No profanity, substitute profanity, or taking the Lord's name in vain.
- Clean romance only: physical affection does not go beyond kissing; no implied bedroom scenes or fade-to-black intimacy beyond kissing.
- Christian/Catholic elements are woven naturally, not preached or bolted on.
- Em dashes are allowed for dialogue interruptions only. Do not use them in narration, asides, emphasis, pacing, or interruptions outside dialogue.
- Use `eye` or `eyes` only for the literal body part. If the meaning is gaze, sight, look, stare, glance, attention, expression, visual direction, or anything nonliteral, use `gaze` or rewrite.
- Short chapters, tight paragraphs, one POV per chapter, no head-hopping.
- Dialogue-driven, cinematic, sensory prose with Patterson-style pacing.
- Minimize `feel`, `felt`, `feels`, `feeling`, `knew`, `wondered`, and `thought`; some instances are legitimate, but prefer stronger verbs, physical behavior, sensory detail, dialogue, or scene context that conveys the same idea without naming the mental state.
- Avoid the nostalgia/habit shorthand pattern `the way he/she used to/does XYZ`; replace it with a specific action, sensory detail, line of dialogue, or concrete remembered moment.
- Preserve Sylvia's voice and pen-name-specific addendums.
- Do not invent canon, physical details, names, places, backstory, dates, injuries, objects, or continuity facts. Ask or draft around uncertainty.

## Before Delivering Fiction Prose

Run a final self-check against:

1. Content standards and clean/Christian boundaries.
2. Pen-name addendum and genre-specific rules.
3. Banned AI-cliche list, hard-zero phrases, and nostalgia/habit shorthand patterns.
4. Limited-use mental-state verbs: `feel`, `felt`, `feels`, `feeling`, `knew`, `wondered`, and `thought`.
5. Dialogue-interruption-only em dash and eye/gaze rules.
6. POV, continuity, chapter hook, paragraph length and voice fit.

If a generated passage violates any hard rule, revise it before showing the user.

- All pen names use CMOS/Oxford comma in lists of three or more items unless Sylvia explicitly requests a non-CMOS exception for a specific project.
