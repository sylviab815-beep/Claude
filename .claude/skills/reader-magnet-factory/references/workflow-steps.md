- **Cross-pollinate pen names** — Attract readers from one pen name to another

### Question 7: Series or Standalone?
**"Is this magnet for a series or a standalone book?"**

- **Standalone book** — The magnet complements a single title

## Phase 2: Catalog Analysis & Magnet Strategy

### If Manuscripts Were Uploaded
Read the uploaded manuscript(s). Analyze:
- **Genre and subgenre** — confirm and get specific
- **Tone and voice** — extract the author's writing style, humor, emotional register
- **Key characters** — who do readers love? who do they want more of?
- **Series hooks** — what threads are left open that readers are curious about?
- **World elements** — what parts of the world are unexplored?
- **Reader desires** — what would a fan of this book want MORE of?

### Magnet Strategy Recommendation
Based on the analysis, recommend **3 magnet options** ranked by effectiveness:

For each option:
- **Magnet type** (from magnet-types.md)
- **Title**
- **Premise** (2-3 sentences)
- **Why this works** (how it connects to the paid catalog, what reader desire it fulfills)
- **Estimated conversion rate** (low/medium/high based on genre norms)
- **Effort level** (how long to produce)
- **Best for** (which goal from Question 4 this serves)

Present via AskUserQuestion — user picks one or combines elements.

## Phase 3: Write the Reader Magnet

### Pre-Write Setup
- Match the author's voice EXACTLY (from uploaded manuscripts or described tone)
- Ensure the magnet connects to the paid catalog (characters, world, tone, promises)
- Plan the "hook into paid books" moment — where does the reader think "I need to buy the full book/series"?

### Writing Rules
1. **The magnet must stand alone.** It must be satisfying on its own. A reader who never buys another book should still feel they got value.
2. **The magnet must create desire for more.** By the end, the reader should WANT the paid books. Not through cliffhangers or withholding — through making them love the characters/world enough to want more time with them.
3. **Match the paid books' quality.** This is the reader's FIRST experience with the author. If the magnet is sloppy, they'll never buy.
4. **Match the heat level / darkness level / tone of the paid books.** A sweet magnet for a spicy series loses readers. A dark magnet for a cozy series scares them off.
5. **Include a natural "what to read next" moment.** Not a hard sell — a character mention, a plot thread, a world element that the reader will recognize in the paid book.

### For Fiction Magnets (stories, novellas, bonus scenes)
Write the complete story/scene following the same prose quality standards as the genre's Author skill. Apply voice matching, genre-specific craft rules, and AI-ism removal.

### For Non-Fiction Magnets (guides, recipes, world bibles)
- Format for visual appeal (headers, sections, maybe illustrations described)
- Make it feel like a GIFT, not a brochure
- Include personality — this should sound like the author, not a Wikipedia entry
- Embed catalog mentions naturally ("In [Book Title], when [character] visits [location], they're eating at the same café described on page 12 of this guide")

Save the magnet as .docx.

## Phase 4: Landing Page Copy

Write the landing page copy that converts visitors into subscribers.

### Landing Page Structure
1. **Headline** — What the reader GETS (not what they sign up for). "Get [Title] free" or a benefit-driven headline.
2. **Subheadline** — One sentence adding specificity. Genre signal + emotional promise.
3. **Cover image description** — (for the author to create or commission). Describe what the magnet cover should look like.
4. **Description paragraph** — 3-5 sentences. What's the story/content about? Why would a reader want it? What emotional experience does it deliver?
5. **Social proof line** — Template for adding review quotes or subscriber count later.
6. **CTA button text** — Not "Subscribe" — something genre-appropriate. "Send Me the Story" / "Get Your Free Copy" / "Join the Mystery" / "Enter the World"
7. **Below-fold content** — Brief author bio, what they'll receive (the magnet + future emails about new releases), and a privacy reassurance.

### Platform-Specific Versions
Generate copy optimized for:
- **BookFunnel landing page** (shorter, book-focused)
- **StoryOrigin landing page** (similar to BookFunnel)
- **Author website opt-in** (can be longer, more personality)

Save as .html with clean semantic formatting.

## Phase 5: Email Sequence

### Email 0: Delivery Email
The email that delivers the magnet. This is NOT just a download link — it's the first impression.

**Structure:**
- **Subject line** — Exciting, personal, genre-appropriate. Not "Here's your download."
- **Opening** — Warm, enthusiastic, personality-forward. "You just made my day by signing up."
- **The download link** — Clear, prominent, impossible to miss
- **What they just got** — 1-2 sentences about the magnet that build excitement to read it
- **What's coming next** — Brief preview of what they'll get from being on the list (new releases, exclusive content, deals)
- **Sign-off** — Personal, warm, genre-appropriate. First name, not "The [Pen Name] Team"

### Email 1: The Connection Email (sent 2-3 days after delivery)
**Goal:** Build a personal connection. Make the reader feel like they know the author.

**Structure:**
- **Subject line** — Conversational, curious. "Did you finish [Magnet Title]?" or "The story behind [character/book]"
- **Opening** — Check in on the magnet. Hope they enjoyed it.
- **The story** — Share something personal about writing the book/series. Why this story matters. An anecdote that reveals personality.
- **The bridge** — Naturally mention the paid book. Not a hard sell — a genuine "if you liked the magnet, you'll love this because..."
- **CTA** — Soft. Link to the book. "Check it out if you're curious" energy, not "BUY NOW."

### Email 2: The Value Email (sent 3-4 days after Email 1)
**Goal:** Deliver value. Prove the list is worth staying on.

**Structure:**
- **Subject line** — Benefit-driven. "3 [genre] books I can't stop recommending" or "[Character] almost didn't exist — here's why"
- **Content** — Something genuinely useful or entertaining: book recommendations in the genre, behind-the-scenes content, a bonus scene, writing process insight, or a curated list.
- **The embed** — The paid book is mentioned naturally within the value content (not as a separate pitch).
- **CTA** — "Reply and tell me [question]" — engagement builds deliverability.

### Email 3: The Catalog Email (sent 3-4 days after Email 2)
**Goal:** Show the full catalog. Make the purchase path clear.

**Structure:**
- **Subject line** — "Not sure what to read next?" or "Your reading guide for [pen name]"
- **Content** — Walk through the catalog with personality. Not a list of links — a guided tour. "Start here if you like [trope]. Start here if you want [emotion]. Start here if you loved [magnet character]."
- **Series order** — If applicable, clear reading order with brief hooks for each.
- **The social proof** — Mention reviews, ratings, or reader messages (template if none exist yet).
- **CTA** — Direct. "Grab [Book 1] and see where it all begins."

Save all emails as a single .docx with clear section headers.

## Phase 6: Strategy Document & Delivery

### Magnet Strategy Document
Compile everything into a strategy document:

1. **Magnet Summary** — What was created, why this type was chosen, how it connects to the catalog
2. **Distribution Setup** — Step-by-step for uploading to BookFunnel/StoryOrigin
3. **Email Automation Setup** — Step-by-step for setting up the welcome sequence in their email platform (MailerLite, ConvertKit, etc.)
4. **Landing Page Deployment** — Where to host, how to set up
5. **Promotion Plan** — Where to share the magnet (BookFunnel promos, social media, author website, book back matter, swap groups)
6. **Measurement** — What to track (signup rate, open rates, click rates, conversion to purchase) and what good numbers look like
7. **Refresh Schedule** — When to update the magnet (seasonal refreshes, after new book launches, when conversion drops)

### File Delivery
Save to user's workspace folder:
1. **[Pen Name] — Reader Magnet Strategy.docx**
2. **[Title] — Reader Magnet.docx** (the actual magnet content)
3. **[Pen Name] — Landing Page Copy.html**
4. **[Pen Name] — Welcome Email Sequence.docx** (delivery email + 3 sequence emails)
