# The Funnel Integrity System

This is the signature guardrail for the Reader Magnet Factory. It exists because AI creates disconnected content — a magnet that doesn't lead anywhere, emails that sound like a different person wrote them, landing pages that promise what the magnet doesn't deliver, and welcome sequences that never actually sell anything.

## The Problem

Left unchecked, AI will:
- Write a reader magnet with no connection to the paid catalog (just a random free story)
- Match the genre but not the author's specific voice
- Create a landing page that oversells or undersells the magnet
- Write emails in generic "newsletter" voice instead of the author's personality
- Never actually bridge to a purchase — the sequence just... ends
- Make the magnet too good (a complete experience with no desire for more) or too thin (a teaser that feels like bait)
- Forget that different genres have completely different reader magnet expectations
- Treat every email the same — no escalation from connection → value → catalog → purchase

## The System

### Rule 1: Every Piece Connects to the Next

The funnel is a CHAIN. Each link must pull the reader forward:

**Magnet → Creates desire for more** (through characters, world, unresolved threads, or pure quality)
**Delivery Email → Builds excitement to READ the magnet** (not just "here's your link")
**Email 1 → Builds personal connection to the AUTHOR** (why they should care about this person's other books)
**Email 2 → Delivers value that PROVES the list is worth staying on** (while naturally mentioning paid work)
**Email 3 → Makes the purchase path CLEAR** (guided tour of the catalog, not a list of links)

**Per-Piece Check:** Does this piece make the reader want the NEXT thing? If any link is weak, the chain breaks.

### Rule 2: Voice Match Is Non-Negotiable

The reader magnet is the reader's FIRST experience with the author. If the voice doesn't match the paid books, the reader feels deceived.

**What this means:**
- If the author writes snarky urban fantasy, the magnet AND emails must be snarky
- If the author writes tender romance, the magnet AND emails must be warm
- The delivery email shouldn't sound like a corporation. It should sound like the author texting a friend.
- The landing page copy should match the genre's emotional register — dark romance landing pages don't use exclamation marks and sunshine language
- Welcome sequence emails should feel like the same person who wrote the book

**Voice Consistency Check:** Read the magnet's first page, the landing page headline, and the delivery email subject line in sequence. Do they sound like the same person? If not → rewrite the outliers.

### Rule 3: The Magnet Serves the Catalog (Not the Other Way Around)

The magnet is not a standalone product. It's a strategic tool that exists to sell books.

**What this means:**
- The magnet should feature characters, world elements, or themes from the PAID catalog
- If it's a prequel, it should create questions that the paid book answers
- If it's a bonus epilogue, it should make readers who haven't read the series want to start
- If it's a character guide or recipe book, it should reference specific scenes and moments from the paid books
- The "read next" path should be OBVIOUS without being pushy

**Catalog Connection Check:** After reading the magnet, would a reader naturally think "I want more of this"? Is the path to the first paid book clear?

### Rule 4: Genre Expectations Drive Everything

Reader magnet conventions vary DRASTICALLY by genre. What works in romance will fail in horror.

**Romance readers expect:** A complete romantic experience. A meet-cute, chemistry, and at least a HFN. Bonus epilogues and alternate POV chapters are gold.

**Cozy mystery readers expect:** A charming introduction to the sleuth and setting. Recipe books, character guides, and short prequel mysteries work well.

**Dark romance readers expect:** Something that matches the darkness level. A sweet freebie for a dark series loses subscribers instantly.

**Thriller readers expect:** A tight, fast read that shows the author can build tension. A short prequel or a different case featuring the same protagonist.

**Urban fantasy readers expect:** Voice-forward content. A case or adventure that shows the protagonist's personality and the world. They're signing up for the narrator as much as the story.

**Horror readers expect:** Something genuinely unsettling. A "horror" magnet that's actually just mildly creepy will lose the reader.

**Cozy fantasy readers expect:** Warmth, charm, and a taste of the cozy world. Maybe a crafting scene, a tavern evening, a character introduction.

**Per-Genre Check:** Does this magnet meet the SPECIFIC expectations of readers in this genre? Not just "a free story" but "the right KIND of free story for these readers"?

### Rule 5: The Emails Escalate

The welcome sequence is NOT four versions of the same email. Each one has a distinct purpose and escalates toward purchase.

**Escalation Pattern:**
- Delivery: Excitement and logistics (get them reading)
- Email 1: Connection and curiosity (get them caring about the author)
- Email 2: Value and trust (prove the list is worth their time)
- Email 3: Catalog and purchase (make buying easy and natural)

**What escalation means:**
- Each email asks for slightly more investment from the reader
- The author reveals slightly more of themselves each time
- The mentions of paid books become slightly more direct each time
- The CTA becomes slightly stronger each time (from "check it out" to "grab your copy")

**Escalation Check:** Read the four emails in sequence. Does each one feel like the natural next step? Does the ask grow? Does the reader feel READY for the catalog email by the time it arrives?

### Rule 6: The Landing Page Converts, Not Convinces

Landing page copy for reader magnets has ONE job: get the signup. It's not a blog post. It's not a book description. It's a value exchange.

**What this means:**
- Headline = what they GET (not what they give)
- Description = emotional promise (not plot summary)
- CTA = action-oriented and genre-appropriate
- Trust signals = brief and real (not paragraphs of persuasion)
- Above the fold = everything they need to decide
- Below the fold = for the skeptics who need more

**The 3-Second Test:** If someone glanced at this page for 3 seconds, would they know: (1) what they get, (2) that it's free, and (3) that it's for THEM (genre signal)?

## Final Delivery Audit

Before delivering the complete package, check:

1. **Voice Consistency**: Magnet → delivery email → sequence emails → landing page. Same voice throughout?
2. **Catalog Connection**: Does the magnet create desire for the paid books? Is the path clear?
3. **Genre Match**: Does every piece meet the specific expectations of THIS genre's readers?
4. **Escalation**: Do the emails build from connection to purchase naturally?
5. **Landing Page**: Does it pass the 3-second test?
6. **Quality**: Is the magnet good enough that readers would pay for it? (This is the standard — free doesn't mean low quality.)
