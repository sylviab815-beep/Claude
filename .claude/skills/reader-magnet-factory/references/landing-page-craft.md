# Landing Page Copy Craft

## The 3-Second Rule
A visitor decides in 3 seconds whether to stay. In those 3 seconds they must know:
1. What they GET (a free book/story/guide)
2. That it's FREE
3. That it's for THEM (genre signal)

Everything above the fold serves these three answers.

## Structure

### Above the Fold (what they see without scrolling)

**Headline:** What they get. Not clever — clear.
- "Get [Title] FREE" (simple, effective)
- "A free [genre] [type] for readers who love [trope/vibe]" (genre-targeted)
- "[Compelling question that the magnet answers]" (curiosity-driven)

**Subheadline:** One sentence. Emotional promise + genre signal.
- "A [length] [genre] [story/novella/guide] about [hook]"
- "For readers who love [comp authors] and [specific trope]"

**Cover Image:** Describe for the author to create. Should look like a real book cover, not a generic freebie.

**Email Input + CTA Button:**
- Simple form: just email (name optional, reduces friction)
- Button text: genre-appropriate and action-oriented
  - Romance: "Send Me the Story" / "Get Your Free Romance"
  - Thriller: "Get the Free Thriller" / "Send It Now"
  - Cozy: "Join the Mystery" / "Get Your Free Cozy"
  - UF: "Send Me the Adventure" / "Get the Free Story"
  - Horror: "Get the Free Horror" / "Send Me the Darkness"
  - General: "Get Your Free Copy" / "Send It to Me"

### Below the Fold (for people who need more)

**Description:** 3-5 sentences. What's the story/content about? Written in the author's voice, not marketing speak. Should create emotional anticipation.

**What You'll Get:**
- The free [book/story/guide]
- New release alerts (only when something new comes out — no spam promise)
- Exclusive bonus content (if applicable)
- Genre-specific perks ("Early access to cover reveals" for romance, "First chapter previews" for thriller)

**Author Bio:** 2-3 sentences. Personality-forward. Not a resume — a vibe check. "I write [genre] about [themes] and I have strong opinions about [genre-specific thing]."

**Trust Signal:** One line. "Join [X] readers" or a short review quote or "As featured in [if applicable]."

**Privacy Note:** One line. "Your email is safe. No spam, ever. Unsubscribe anytime."

## Platform-Specific Notes

### BookFunnel
- Shorter copy (the platform handles delivery mechanics)
- Focus on the book description and genre signals
- Cover image is prominent — describe a good one
- CTA is "Claim Your Copy" or similar

### StoryOrigin
- Similar to BookFunnel
- Can include more author personality
- Group promo pages have limited space — optimize for brevity

### Author Website
- More room for personality and brand building
- Can include longer author bio, testimonials, preview text
- Embed the form directly (MailerLite/ConvertKit embed code)
- Consider a dedicated landing page (no navigation menu — reduce exits)

## Conversion Optimization Notes
- One action per page (sign up). No other links, no menu, no distractions.
- Mobile-first design (most readers browse on phones)
- Fast load time (no heavy images or scripts)
- Test different headlines if traffic allows (A/B testing)
- Social proof increases conversion 15-30% — add it when you have it

## What NOT to Do
- Don't describe the magnet as "free" more than twice (once in headline, once near CTA)
- Don't list everything on the newsletter (too much commitment feels spammy)
- Don't use stock photos — a real cover image converts better
- Don't make the form long (email only, maybe first name)
- Don't promise things you won't deliver (weekly content if you email monthly)
- Don't forget the genre signal — a romance reader landing on a page that looks like thriller will bounce instantly
