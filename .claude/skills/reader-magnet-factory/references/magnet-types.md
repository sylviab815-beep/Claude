# Reader Magnet Types — Complete Guide

## Fiction Magnets

### Prequel Novella (15,000-25,000 words)
**Best for:** Series. Introduces the protagonist before Book 1.
**Why it works:** Readers meet the character, fall in love, then want the full story.
**Genre fit:** Excellent for UF, thriller, cozy mystery, romance series.
**Danger:** Can feel like a "deleted prologue" if not self-contained. Must have its own arc.
**Connection method:** Ends at a moment that makes readers want Book 1.

### Bonus Epilogue (2,000-5,000 words)
**Best for:** Romance (any sub). Shows the couple after the HEA.
**Why it works:** Romance readers are OBSESSED with "after the book" content.
**Genre fit:** Romance, dark romance, romantasy. Less effective for mystery/thriller/horror.
**Danger:** Can feel pointless if nothing happens. Needs a small conflict or revelation.
**Connection method:** References events from the book, making non-readers curious.

### Alternate POV Chapter (3,000-8,000 words)
**Best for:** Dual-POV romances, thrillers, any book with a compelling secondary character.
**Why it works:** Readers get the scene they love from a perspective they've been curious about.
**Genre fit:** Romance (the love interest's POV), thriller (the villain's POV), UF (the sidekick's POV).
**Danger:** Can be boring if the scene wasn't interesting from the original POV. Pick the RIGHT scene.
**Connection method:** Reveals something new about the character that enriches re-reading the paid book.

### Origin Story (5,000-15,000 words)
**Best for:** Characters with mysterious backgrounds. UF protagonists, dark romance heroes, thriller investigators.
**Why it works:** Readers want to know HOW the character became who they are.
**Genre fit:** UF, dark romance, thriller, paranormal. Less effective for contemporary romance.
**Danger:** Can undermine mystery if the character's past is better left ambiguous.
**Connection method:** Answers one question and raises two more.

### Short Story in the Same World (5,000-10,000 words)
**Best for:** Series with rich world-building. Different characters, same universe.
**Why it works:** Expands the world without requiring the reader to commit to a full novel.
**Genre fit:** UF, fantasy, cozy mystery (different case, same town), horror (different victim, same threat).
**Danger:** Can feel disconnected if the world elements don't overlap enough.
**Connection method:** Features locations, secondary characters, or events mentioned in the paid books.

### Deleted Scene (2,000-5,000 words)
**Best for:** Books where scenes were cut for pacing but fans would love them.
**Why it works:** Feels exclusive — "you're getting something the published book doesn't have."
**Genre fit:** Any genre, but works best when the deleted scene is emotionally significant.
**Danger:** Might have been cut for a reason. Only use genuinely good scenes.
**Connection method:** Directly connects to the published book's events.

### Seasonal Short (3,000-8,000 words)
**Best for:** Pen names with established readership. Holiday or seasonal content.
**Why it works:** Timely, shareable, refreshable. A Christmas cozy mystery every December. A Halloween horror every October.
**Genre fit:** Cozy mystery, romance, cozy fantasy, horror.
**Danger:** Has a shelf life. Needs refreshing or rotating.
**Connection method:** Features beloved characters in a seasonal setting.

### Crossover Teaser (5,000-10,000 words)
**Best for:** Authors with multiple pen names or series.
**Why it works:** Introduces readers from one series to characters from another.
**Genre fit:** Any genre where two series could plausibly overlap.
**Danger:** Can confuse readers if the crossover is forced.
**Connection method:** Character from Series A meets character from Series B.

## Non-Fiction Magnets

### Character Guide (3,000-8,000 words)
**Best for:** Series with large casts. Cozy mystery, UF, fantasy.
**Why it works:** Readers love reference material for their favorite series.
**Genre fit:** Cozy mystery, UF, romantasy, cozy fantasy. Less effective for standalone thrillers.
**Danger:** Can be dry if it reads like a database. Needs personality.
**Connection method:** Entries reference specific scenes and moments from the books.

### Recipe Book / Craft Guide (3,000-10,000 words)
**Best for:** Cozy mystery and cozy fantasy where food/crafting is central.
**Why it works:** Readers of cozy genres love lifestyle content connected to the story world.
**Genre fit:** Cozy mystery (the protagonist's recipes), cozy fantasy (tavern recipes, potion recipes).
**Danger:** Must be actually usable — real recipes that work, not just flavor text.
**Connection method:** Each recipe is tied to a specific scene or character in the books.

### World Guide / Map (variable)
**Best for:** Fantasy, UF, any series with complex world-building.
**Why it works:** Fans want to explore the world beyond the story.
**Genre fit:** UF (city guide), fantasy (world map with notes), horror (location history).
**Danger:** Can spoil future books if not carefully curated.
**Connection method:** References events from published books while hinting at unexplored areas.

### Reading Order Guide + First Chapter Sampler (5,000-10,000 words)
**Best for:** Authors with large backlists or multiple series.
**Why it works:** Removes the "where do I start?" barrier.
**Genre fit:** Any genre with 5+ published titles.
**Danger:** Can feel like a catalog if not done with personality.
**Connection method:** Each entry has a personalized recommendation and the first chapter.

## Genre-Specific Recommendations

| Genre | Top Magnet | Runner-Up | Avoid |
|-------|-----------|-----------|-------|
| Contemporary Romance | Bonus epilogue | Alternate POV | World guide (no world to guide) |
| Dark Romance | Origin story (the hero) | Alternate POV (his perspective) | Anything sweet-toned |
| Cozy Mystery | Prequel mystery | Recipe book | Bonus epilogue (mysteries don't have HEAs the same way) |
| Cozy Fantasy | Tavern/crafting guide | Short story (same world) | Character guide (too early for new readers) |
| Thriller | Prequel case | Short story (same detective) | Recipe book (wrong genre) |
| Urban Fantasy | Prequel or origin story | City guide | Bonus epilogue (UF readers want more story, not after-story) |
| Horror | Short story (same threat) | Origin of the threat | Character guide (horror characters aren't "beloved") |
| Historical Romance | Alternate POV | Bonus epilogue | World guide (it's real history) |
| Romantasy | Prequel or origin story | World guide/map | Recipe book (unless crafting is central) |
