# AI Names & Places Avoidance List for Thrillers

These names, places, and patterns are overused in AI-generated thriller fiction. NEVER use any of them.

## Character Names — NEVER USE

**Female protagonists**: Sarah, Kate, Claire, Emma, Hannah, Rachel, Megan, Jessica, Olivia (as "every-thriller heroine"). Avoid the "generic competent woman" default.

**Male protagonists**: Jack, Mark, Tom, David, James, Michael, Daniel, Ryan (as "generic thriller guy"). Avoid the "everyman" default.

**Surnames for "tough" characters**: Blackwood, Grayson, Hawkins, Sterling, Cross, Stone, Drake, Shaw, Wolfe, Hunter, Raven, Fox, Steele, Knight, Archer

**Antagonist names**: Dr. Marcus/Elena/Viktor (the brilliant villain), anything Eastern European used as shorthand for "sinister"

**Detective/Agent names**: Detective [Hard Surname], Agent [Hard Surname] — avoid the sound-tough formula

**Name Patterns to Avoid**:
- Single-syllable "hard" surnames for protagonists (Chase, Quinn, Steele, Drake, Stone)
- Surnames that sound like they belong on a thriller cover rather than in a phone book
- First names that are "accessible" to the point of invisibility (Sarah, Jack, Kate, Tom)
- Using ethnic names as shorthand for "exotic threat"

## Place Names — NEVER USE

Blackwood Estates, Ravenswood, Oakbrook, Pinecrest, Willowdale, Westmore, Shadowbrook, Graystone Manor, Thornfield, Darkmore

**Formulas to Avoid**:
- [Dark/Ominous] + [Residential] (Blackwood, Darkmore, Shadowbrook)
- [Pleasant] + [Residential] for ironic contrast (Sunnyside, Pleasant Valley, Harmony Lane)
- "The quiet town of [Name] where nothing bad ever happens" setup
- Any place name that telegraphs "something terrible will happen here"

## Title Words — AVOID

Silent, Dark, Hidden, Twisted, Shattered, Broken, Gone, Missing, Girl, Woman, Wife, Lie, Lies, Truth, Secret, Last, Final, Dead, Blood, Night, Stranger, Watch, Behind, Beneath, Locked, Nobody, Someone, Forgotten, Buried, Perfect, Other, After, Before, Quiet, Still, Deep, Close, Next, Every, Never

## Business/Organization Names — AVOID

- "Meridian Corporation" or any corporate name with Meridian, Nexus, Apex, Zenith, Cipher, Sentinel
- "[Greek/Latin word] Pharmaceuticals"
- "The [Abstract Noun] Institute"

## What to Use Instead

- **Character names**: Use names that feel demographically accurate for the character's age, location, and background. A 43-year-old corporate lawyer from Houston is not "Kate Sterling." She might be "Priya Mehta" or "Connie Becerra" or "Denise Okafor." Make names specific to who the character IS.
- **Place names**: Use real neighborhoods in real cities, or create fictional places that sound like real places. "The Meadows" (a housing development). "Braddock County." "The old Kessler building on Route 18." Specificity creates reality.
- **Titles**: Use titles that are specific to your story — a phrase from the text, a location, a detail. "The Eleven O'Clock Rule." "Building 4." "The Glass House." Avoid any title that could be on 50 other thrillers.
- **Organizations**: Real companies have boring names. "Landon Mercer Financial." "MedArc Solutions." "Bern & Associates." The more mundane the name, the more real it feels.
- **Test**: If the name sounds like it belongs on a movie poster, change it. Real life has boring names. That's what makes them real.
