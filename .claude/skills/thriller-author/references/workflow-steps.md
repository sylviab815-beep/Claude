## Phase 2: Outline Generation (if needed)

### Step 2a: Concept Development

Generate 3 concepts. Each includes:
- Title
- One-line hook (create instant dread)
- Protagonist: name, occupation, flaw, blind spot, what they stand to lose
- Antagonist: role, method, connection to protagonist, information advantage
- The Disruption: what goes wrong
- The Stakes: what the protagonist will lose if they fail (specific, personal)
- The Ticking Clock (if applicable)
- Twist structure: what the reader thinks is happening vs. what IS happening
- The Climax Connection: how the protagonist's flaw/blind spot relates to the resolution
- Setting: where this takes place and why that setting amplifies tension

Present all 3. Use AskUserQuestion to pick.

### Step 2b: Full Outline

Chapter-by-chapter including:
- Chapter number and title
- POV character
- Summary (3-5 sentences)
- Tension level (1-10)
- Information released / Information withheld
- Twist seed planted or paid off
- Tone, goal, complication
- Stakes escalation
- Ticking clock status

Plus:
- **Information Release Schedule** (full table)
- **Tension Escalation Map** (chart)
- **Twist Setup Inventory** (what clues are planted where)
- **Misdirection Log** (what false beliefs are active when)

**Present and ask for approval.**

## Phase 3: Manuscript Writing

### Step 3a: Pre-Writing Setup

1. Read `references/tension-architecture.md` — internalize information control and tension rules
2. Read `references/prose-craft.md` — internalize thriller writing standards
3. If style guide exists, keep active
4. Note the starting tension level — this is baseline

### Step 3b: Write Each Chapter

For each chapter:

1. **Check information budget** — what does the reader learn, what stays hidden, what misdirection is active?
2. **Check tension level** — where are we on the ratchet? Escalation, relief valve, or revelation?
3. **Check twist setup** — does this chapter plant or pay off a twist seed? If so, bury the clue.
4. **If dual/multi POV**: confirm whose chapter. Manage information asymmetry (what does THIS character know vs. the other? vs. the reader?).
5. **Write the chapter** following:
   - Information budget — no early reveals
   - Tension ratchet — never flatline
   - Prose craft rules — tension through implication, dialogue as weapon
   - Style guide (if exists)
   - Chapter must end on a hook, question, revelation, or dread
   - Protagonist earns every insight — no AI-speed deduction
   - Antagonist presence felt even when not on page
6. **Verify**: Would a reader who doesn't know the ending be able to figure it out from this chapter alone? If yes — you've revealed too much.
7. **End check**: Does this chapter end in a way that makes the reader unable to stop? If it ends on resolution — rewrite the ending.

Write chapters sequentially.

### Step 3c: Completeness Verification

- Every chapter: fully written prose, no placeholders
- Target word count per chapter: ~1,250 (novella), ~1,600 (standard), ~1,800 (extended)
- No chapter under 800 words (thriller chapters can be SHORT)
- Total within 10% of target

## Phase 4: Editorial Pipeline

### Step 4a: Tension Architecture Audit

BEFORE other editing:
1. Information control check (budget matches outline)
2. Tension escalation map (ratchet pattern verified)
3. Protagonist competence check (wrong conclusions, dead ends, earned breakthroughs)
4. Twist reverse-engineering (3+ clues per twist)
5. Antagonist presence check (threat felt throughout)
6. Relief valve audit (every low-tension scene plants a seed)
7. Chapter hook check (every chapter ends on tension)

Fix all issues.

### Step 4b: AI-Ism Revision Pass

Read `references/revision-guide.md`:
- Remove robotic phrasing
- Fix POV breaks (critical in unreliable narrator)
- Sharpen dialogue — remove therapy-speak, add subtext
- Improve chapter endings — every one is a hook
- Replace telling with showing — especially for dread and paranoia

### Step 4c: Developmental Edit

Assess:
- Pacing (does tension escalate? does the mid-book hold?)
- Information control (is the reader where they should be at each point?)
- Character (is the protagonist capable but limited? is the antagonist formidable?)
- Twists (do they hold up? are they set up properly?)
- Stakes (are they personal, specific, escalating?)
- Atmosphere (does every scene feel unsafe?)
- Genre compliance (is it a THRILLER? Would a thriller reader be satisfied?)

Apply top 5 fixes.

### Step 4d: Beta Read

Critical read from a thriller reader:
- Star rating
- The Good (specific tension moments, specific twists, specific dread)
- The Bad (pacing drops, predictable twists, smart protagonist problems, atmosphere breaks)
- Suggestions (ranked structural fixes)

Apply top 3.

### Step 4e: Final Tension Check

1. Information control holds — no leaks
2. Tension ratchets properly — no dead zones
3. Twists survive re-read — clues are there
4. Protagonist is smart but not omniscient
5. Antagonist is formidable throughout
6. Every chapter ends on a hook
7. The ending is satisfying (clean, ambiguous, or twist — but EARNED)
