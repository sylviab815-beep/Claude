# KDP Info Document Template — Thriller

Generate a companion .docx with Amazon KDP metadata.

## Document Structure

### 1. Book Information
- Title, Subtitle, Author, Series Name/Number (if applicable)

### 2. Amazon Book Description
Max 4000 characters. Structure for thrillers:
- Hook line (create immediate dread or curiosity — one sentence)
- Setup (the protagonist's world and what they stand to lose)
- The disruption (what goes wrong)
- Escalation (it's worse than they thought — each answer reveals a bigger question)
- Stakes (what happens if they fail — personal, specific)
- The tease (hint at the twist without revealing it)
- Trope signals woven naturally ("fans of unreliable narrators," "if you loved Gone Girl")
- End with urgency

Use HTML: `<b>`, `<i>`, `<br>`, `<h2>`, `<ul>`, `<li>`

### 3. Amazon Keywords (7 phrases)
- 2 broad genre (e.g., "psychological thriller," "domestic suspense")
- 2 trope keywords (e.g., "unreliable narrator thriller," "missing person suspense")
- 2 niche keywords (e.g., "gaslighting thriller," "corporate conspiracy thriller")
- 1 comp keyword (e.g., "books like Gone Girl")

### 4. BISAC Categories (3)

### 5. Pricing
- Ebook price with reasoning
- Print price (if applicable)
- KU recommendation

### 6. Comparable Titles (3-5)
Real published thrillers with specific comparisons.

### 7. Target Audience
- Age range, reading preferences
- What draws them (the twist, the dread, the pace, the psychology)
- Comp readers

### 8. Cover Image Prompt
- Dark, atmospheric, moody
- Symbolic imagery (not literal scenes — an object, a silhouette, a texture)
- Color palette (dark: navy, black, deep red; psychological: muted, desaturated; domestic: deceptively bright with one wrong element)
- Typography (bold sans-serif or distressed type, metallic accents)
- 1600x2560px, 2:3 ratio

### 9. Content Warnings (if applicable)
For dark/extreme intensity: list specific content warnings honestly.

### 10. Series Marketing Notes (if applicable)
- Reading order, standalone entry points
- Whether each book follows the same protagonist or different
- Pricing strategy
