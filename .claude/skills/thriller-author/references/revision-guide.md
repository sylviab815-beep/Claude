# Revision Guide for Thriller Manuscripts

Full editorial pipeline: AI-ism revision, tension architecture audit, developmental edit, beta read, targeted revision.

---

## Stage 1: AI-Ism Revision Pass

### 1. Structure, Pacing, and Flow
- Verify chapter length stays SHORT (thriller chapters: 800-1,800 words)
- Kill manufactured cliffhangers that don't connect to anything ("Little did I know...")
- Every chapter ending must be a hook, question, revelation, or dread beat
- Remove philosophical wrap-ups between chapters
- Verify pacing accelerates in Act 3 — chapters should get SHORTER as tension peaks

### 2. AI Artifact Removal
- Eliminate AI references and prompt language
- Fix POV breaks (critical for unreliable narrators — the reader can't see what the narrator can't)
- Replace robotic connectors
- Remove clinical emotion descriptions — dread is physical, not labeled
- Rewrite "she felt a chill" / "a sense of foreboding" into specific, grounded detail

### 3. Dialogue Polish
- Remove formal/therapeutic dialogue ("I need you to understand...")
- Sharpen interrogation scenes — questions should be weapons
- Add subtext to every important conversation
- Differentiate truth-telling from lying in character speech patterns
- Remove excessive name usage
- Break up overly articulate exchanges — stressed people don't speak in paragraphs

### 4. Narrative Authenticity
- Replace forced metaphors
- Simplify inflated sentences — thriller prose should be lean
- Remove author explanations masquerading as narrator observation
- Keep sensory detail tight — one precise detail beats three general ones
- Verify the narrator's voice is consistent (especially critical for unreliable narrators)

### 5. Crutch Phrase Sweep

| Pattern | Fix |
|---|---|
| "A chill ran down my spine" | Show the specific physical reaction |
| "Something felt off" | Name the specific thing that's wrong |
| "I couldn't shake the feeling" | Show the behavior the feeling causes |
| "My blood ran cold" | Physical specificity — what does the character actually feel? |
| "I knew in that moment" | Show the realization through action or thought |
| "Little did I know" | Delete. The reader doesn't need meta-narration. |
| "My heart hammered" (over 4x) | Vary the physical manifestations |
| "I held my breath" (over 3x) | Different fear responses |
| "Time seemed to slow" | Describe the specific perceptual shift |
| "The silence was deafening" | What IS the character hearing? |

### 6. Thriller-Specific AI Patterns

| AI Pattern | Fix |
|---|---|
| "The truth would shatter everything" | Show the specific consequence |
| "Nothing could have prepared me for..." | Just show the thing |
| "The walls felt like they were closing in" | Ground the claustrophobia in spatial detail |
| "Everything I thought I knew was a lie" | Name the specific false belief and the specific truth |
| "I could feel their eyes on me" | What makes the protagonist think they're being watched? |
| "[Antagonist] smiled, but it didn't reach their eyes" | Find a specific, fresh description |

## Stage 2: Tension Architecture Audit

Run the full audit from `references/tension-architecture.md`:

1. **Information Control**: Budget matches outline for every chapter. No leaks, no skipped plants.
2. **Tension Escalation**: Map 1-10 per chapter. Verify ratchet pattern. No flatlines. No extended drops.
3. **Protagonist Competence**: Wrong conclusions drawn, dead ends hit, breakthroughs earned through character.
4. **Twist Integrity**: 3+ clues per twist, all visible on re-read. Reverse-engineering test passes.
5. **Antagonist Presence**: Threat felt throughout — directly or through effects.
6. **Relief Valve Quality**: Every low-tension scene plants a seed.
7. **Chapter Hooks**: Every chapter ends on tension, not resolution.

Fix all issues.

## Stage 3: Developmental Edit

### Pacing Assessment
- Does Act 1 hook within the first 2-3 chapters?
- Does Act 2 maintain momentum? (The mid-book is the most common failure point.)
- Does the midpoint reversal genuinely change the game?
- Does Act 3 accelerate to an inevitable climax?
- Are chapters short enough to maintain the "one more chapter" engine?

### Character Assessment
- Is the protagonist smart but limited? Can the reader see their blind spot?
- Is the antagonist genuinely formidable with a specific method and motive?
- Are supporting characters distinct and functional (each has a purpose)?
- If unreliable narrator: is the unreliability consistent and fair?

### Stakes Assessment
- Are the stakes personal and specific? Can the reader name what the protagonist stands to lose?
- Do the stakes ESCALATE across the book?
- Is there a point of no return where the protagonist can't walk away?

### Information & Twists
- Does the information release schedule create proper suspense?
- Are red herrings convincing and satisfyingly explained?
- Do twists hold up on re-read?
- Is the reader in the right place at each point — ahead of the protagonist sometimes, behind sometimes, never synced?

### Atmosphere
- Does every scene feel slightly unsafe?
- Are settings used to amplify tension?
- Is time pressure maintained?
- Does the prose create the right sensory experience for the chosen intensity level?

### Genre Compliance
- Is it a THRILLER? Would a thriller reader be satisfied?
- Does it match the chosen subgenre's conventions?
- Is the ending satisfying for the chosen type (clean, ambiguous, twist)?

Apply top 5 fixes.

## Stage 4: Beta Read

Critical read from someone who reads thrillers:

1. **Star Rating** (honest)
2. **The Good** — specific tension moments, specific twists, specific dread. Where did they genuinely not want to stop reading?
3. **The Bad**:
   - Pacing drops (where did they put the book down?)
   - Predictable moments (what did they see coming?)
   - Protagonist problems (too smart, too stupid, unmotivated choices)
   - Dialogue issues (formal, flat, missing subtext)
   - Twist problems (saw it coming, came from nowhere, doesn't hold up)
   - Atmosphere breaks (scenes that didn't feel unsafe)
   - Information problems (confused, felt cheated, figured it out too early)
4. **Suggestions** — ranked structural fixes

Apply top 3.

---

## Stage 5: Final Tension & Integrity Check

1. Information control holds from start to finish
2. Tension never flatlines — the ratchet works
3. Every twist survives the reverse-engineering test
4. The protagonist earns every breakthrough
5. The antagonist is formidable and present throughout
6. Every chapter ends on a hook
7. The ending is earned and satisfying
8. The book matches the intensity level chosen
9. All user choices (subgenre, tropes, twists, POV) are fully delivered

Not ready until this passes clean.
