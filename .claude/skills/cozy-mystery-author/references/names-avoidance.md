# AI Names & Places Avoidance List

These names, places, and patterns are overused in AI-generated fiction. NEVER use any of them.

## Character Names — NEVER USE

**Female**: Elara, Luna, Aurora, Willow, Sage, Ivy, Wren, Ember, Rowan, Juniper, Seraphina, Isolde, Evangeline, Cordelia, Celestia, Dahlia, Lyra, Aria, Brynn, Elowen, Maeve (when paired with magical abilities), Cassandra, Astrid

**Male**: Thorne, Ash, Jasper, Hawthorne, Sterling, Orion, Caspian, Silas (in paranormal), Gideon, Alaric, Dorian, Lucian, Zephyr, Atlas, Beckett, Declan (in fantasy/paranormal), Kai (as mysterious stranger)

**Name Patterns to Avoid**:
- Names ending in -wyn, -ael, -ienne, -elle
- Nature-word names used for "mystical" characters
- Old-fashioned names chosen purely for "quaint cozy" vibes without considering if real people have that name in that generation

## Place Names — NEVER USE

Willowbrook, Maplewood, Ravenwood, Thornfield, Briarwood, Shadowvale, Moonhaven, Starfall, Crystalbrook, Silvermist, Whispering Pines, Enchanted Hollow, Rosemary Lane, Lavender Hill

**Formulas to Avoid**:
- [Tree] + [Landscape Feature] (Elmdale, Oakridge, Birchwood)
- [Mystical Word] + [Geographic Feature] (Moonlake, Starvale, Misthollow)
- [Flower/Herb] + [Cozy Word] (Rosemary Cottage, Lavender Gardens)

## Business Names — NEVER USE

- "The Cozy [Noun]" (The Cozy Kettle, The Cozy Nook)
- "Enchanted [Noun]" (Enchanted Pages, Enchanted Brews)
- "[Name]'s [Whimsical Noun]" when the noun is too on-the-nose
- "The [Adjective] [Animal]" for pubs/inns (The Sleepy Fox, The Wise Owl)
- Anything with Whisper, Shadow, Secret, or Ember in the name

## Book Title Words — AVOID

Whispers, Shadows, Secrets, Embers, Thorns, Binding, Veil, Echo, Cursed, Fated, Shattered, Twisted, Woven, Tangled, Unraveled, Forgotten

## What to Use Instead

- **Real names from real places**: Look at actual small towns. Denton, Millbrook, Harlan, Prescott, Calvert, Winfield.
- **Real people names**: Think about what actual women born in the 1970s-1980s are named. Jennifer, Karen, Michelle, Stephanie, Andrea, Tammy. For younger characters: Brittany, Ashley, Morgan, Taylor.
- **Real business naming patterns**: Businesses are usually named after the owner, the street, or something literal. "Brennan's Bakery" not "The Enchanted Oven."
- **Test**: Could you find this name in a phone book? On Google Maps? If not, it's probably too AI.
