# Revision Guide for Cozy Mystery Manuscripts

This guide covers the full editorial pipeline: AI-ism revision, developmental edit, beta read, and targeted revision.

---

## Stage 1: AI-Ism Revision Pass

Fix the telltale signs of AI-generated prose. Apply ALL of the following across the entire manuscript.

### 1. Structure, Pacing, and Flow

- Preserve chapter length — do not compress or summarize scenes to "tighten"
- Remove philosophical wrap-ups, forced cliffhangers, or end-of-chapter moralizing
- Kill one-line cliché dramatic endings and manufactured mic-drops ("And that's when everything changed." / "Little did I know..." / "Nothing would ever be the same.")
- End chapters mid-scene or on forward momentum — an action, a decision, a discovery, an unresolved problem
- Maintain continuous narrative motion so each chapter pulls the reader into the next

### 2. AI Artifact Removal

- Eliminate any direct AI references
- Remove leftover prompt language
- Cut meta-commentary or author analysis disguised as character thoughts
- Fix POV breaks, perspective slips, or omniscient intrusions
- Replace robotic connectors: "Additionally," "Furthermore," "It's worth noting," "However, it's important to remember"
- Remove clinical descriptions of emotions ("she experienced a complex mixture of relief and apprehension")
- Eliminate artificial precision unless plot-critical ("at exactly 3:47 PM" when no one is checking a clock)
- Rewrite AI-style emotional labeling into lived, experiential prose

### 3. Dialogue Polish

- Replace unnatural or overly formal dialogue with authentic speech
- Reduce overuse of surnames when first names or nicknames fit
- Remove repetitive call-and-response patterns
- Break up overly polished exchanges that feel scripted
- Introduce natural interruptions, overlaps, pauses, and messiness
- Ensure each character maintains a consistent, distinct voice

### 4. Narrative Authenticity

- Replace forced metaphors or overly elaborate descriptions
- Simplify inflated or mechanical sentence structures
- Remove author explanations masquerading as character observation
- Eliminate AI-style transitional phrases
- Keep all sensory detail grounded in the POV character's awareness and limitations

### 5. Crutch Phrase Sweep

Search the full manuscript for these patterns. If ANY appears more than 3 times, rewrite every instance after the third:

| Pattern | Why It's Bad |
|---|---|
| "the air of someone who..." | Tells instead of shows |
| "the expression of someone who..." | Describe the actual expression |
| "a sound that suggested..." | Describe the actual sound |
| "apparently" (over 5 total) | Hedge word — commit to the observation |
| "the kind of [noun] that..." | Distancing — put the reader IN the moment |
| "something about [noun] suggested..." | Vague intuition dump — show the detail |
| "it was clear that..." | If it's clear, just show it |
| "seemed to" / "appeared to" (over 5 total) | Weakens every sentence |
| "couldn't help but..." | Removes character agency |
| "found herself [verb]-ing" | She's doing it — own it |

## Stage 2: Developmental Edit

Assess the manuscript's structural integrity across these categories:

### Mystery Craft
- Is the central mystery compelling and sustained throughout?
- Do all clues appear on-page before the reveal? (fair-play check)
- Are there at least 3 viable suspects with genuine motives?
- Are red herrings convincing and satisfyingly debunked?
- Is the killer's identity surprising but, in retrospect, inevitable?
- Does the reveal use only previously established information?

### Pacing
- Does the opening hook within the first few pages?
- Is the body discovered by chapter 2-3?
- Does the investigation have momentum through the middle?
- Is the dark moment genuinely threatening?
- Does the reveal feel earned and satisfying?
- Does the resolution get enough space (not rushed)?

### Character
- Is the sleuth's voice consistent and distinctive?
- Does each suspect have a unique voice and personality?
- Is the familiar consistent in personality and communication style?
- Are secondary characters dimensional?
- If romance: does the love interest feel like a real person?

### Genre Compliance
- Is the cozy tone maintained even during tense moments?
- Are comfort elements present (food, community, sensory warmth)?
- Is violence off-page?
- Does justice prevail?
- If paranormal: are magical rules consistent?
- If romance: is the pacing appropriate for the book's series position?

### Produce Priority Action Items
Rank the top 5-7 revisions by impact. Apply all of them, rewriting affected chapters as complete prose.

## Stage 3: Beta Read

Read the revised manuscript as a critical beta reader who reads a lot of cozy mysteries, especially the relevant subgenre.

### What to Assess

1. **Star Rating** (out of 5) — honest, not generous. 3-3.5 is normal.

2. **The Good** — specific scenes and dynamics that work. Name chapters and characters. Be detailed: "The scene in Chapter 6 where the hedgehog refuses to uncurl during the interrogation" is useful. "The familiar is fun" is not.

3. **The Bad** — specific problems:
   - Show-vs-tell failures (emotions named, themes stated in dialogue, exposition blocks)
   - Mystery problems (clues from nowhere, reveals that don't play fair, too obvious or too opaque)
   - Pacing issues (chapters that drag, rushed sections)
   - Character issues (inconsistent voices, flat suspects)
   - Dialogue problems (therapy-speak, everyone sounding the same)
   - Tone drift (too dark for cozy, too silly for mystery)
   - If paranormal: magical elements feeling decorative rather than integral
   - If romance: pacing too fast or too slow for series position

4. **Suggestions for Improving** — ranked list of 3-5 specific, actionable structural fixes. Not line edits — structural changes.

### Apply Top 3 Fixes
For each fix, identify which chapters need rewriting. Rewrite as complete prose. Check continuity after changes.

## Stage 4: Procedural & Genre Audit

### Procedural Logic
- Chain of custody: Does the sleuth ever pocket or hide physical evidence? Fix it.
- Law enforcement competence: Is the police character unreasonably stupid? They should be competent but resource-limited.
- Legal plausibility: Are arrests based on actual evidence?
- Confrontation safety: Does the sleuth confront the killer alone with no backup and no reason?
- Timeline consistency: Do travel times and event sequences make sense?

### Genre Drift Check
- Comfort elements present every 3-chapter stretch (food, drink, cozy moments)
- If the book has a familiar: animal does something purely animal at least once every 4 chapters
- If apocalyptic/high-stakes magical threat: at least 25% of chapter time is personal-scale cozy scenes
- Community scenes make the reader feel part of the town

### Suspect Pool Validation
- At least 3 named suspects introduced by Chapter 6 (or equivalent for novel length)
- Each false suspect has at least one genuine piece of evidence pointing to them
- Killer should not be obvious before the 60% mark
- Law enforcement pursues at least one false lead

## Stage 5: Final Outline Adherence Check

This is the LAST thing before delivery. Read `references/outline-adherence.md` and run the full audit:

1. Knowledge State verification per chapter
2. Suspect treatment scan
3. Clue timing check
4. Red herring quality check
5. Backward writing scan
6. Reveal integrity check

The manuscript is not ready for delivery until this audit passes clean.
