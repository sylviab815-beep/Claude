# Women's Fiction Outline

Guide the user through an interactive, step-by-step process to develop a women's fiction story from a seed idea into a fully detailed chapter outline. Every step pauses for user input before proceeding.

Women's fiction is driven by a single question: *Who is this woman becoming?* The central engine is the protagonist's inner transformation — her identity, relationships, wounds, and choices — not a romantic relationship. Romance may exist, but it supports rather than drives the story.

## Core Principles

- **One step at a time**: Present each step's output fully and WAIT for the user's feedback before moving on. Never combine steps or jump ahead.
- **Protagonist-centered**: Everything orbits the protagonist's inner arc. Even ensemble casts and subplots exist to illuminate her transformation.
- **Romance is a subplot, not the spine**: If romance is present, it should complicate or catalyze the protagonist's journey — not replace it.
- **Realistic hope**: Endings should feel earned and honest. Growth, resolution, and a credible new beginning matter more than a guaranteed happily-ever-after.
- **Market-informed** (when available): If the user provides a K-Lytics report, use it to identify marketable themes and reader expectations. Otherwise draw on your own knowledge of the women's fiction market.
- **Detail-oriented outlines**: Every chapter in the final outline must include all required fields (see Chapter Outline Format below).
- **Fresh and original**: All names, places, and titles must avoid overused AI-generated patterns. Read the bundled Names & Places Avoidance List before generating any content.

## Names & Places Avoidance List

Read the file `references/ai-names-avoidance.md` bundled with this skill before generating any story content. It contains comprehensive lists of overused AI-generated names, places, businesses, title words, and descriptive clichés. ALL generated content must avoid these throughout the entire workflow.

## Number & Time Diversity (Anti-7 Bias)

LLMs default to 7 for "random" numbers. Actively avoid this in all generated content — ages, times, dates, quantities, durations. Distribute numbers across all ending digits. After completing any outline, scan for the digit 7 and replace most instances.

## Output Formats

### Protagonist Bio Format (for Novelcrafter)
```
Name:
Age:
Occupation:
Physical Description:
Personality Traits:
Backstory:
The Wound (what hurt her in the past that still shapes her now):
The Lie She Believes (the false belief driving her choices):
The Truth She Needs (what she must accept to grow):
External Goal (what she wants):
Internal Goal (what she needs):
Relationship to Other Characters:
Arc/Growth:
Quirks/Habits:
Voice/Speech Patterns:
```

### Supporting Character Bio Format (for Novelcrafter)
```
Name:
Role in Story:
Relationship to Protagonist:
Key Personality Traits:
Brief Backstory:
Purpose in Plot (how they serve the protagonist's arc):
```

### Chapter Outline Format
Each chapter entry must include:
- **Chapter Number & Title**
- **POV Character** (note if ensemble; otherwise the protagonist)
- **Chapter Summary** (3-5 sentences minimum)
- **Theme Tags** (which themes and life-situation tropes this chapter advances)
- **Chapter Tone**
- **Chapter Goal** (what this chapter accomplishes for the story)
- **Protagonist's Inner State** (where she is on her transformation arc at this point)
- **Complication** (what challenges or disrupts her)
- **Key Relationship Moment** (which relationship is activated or changed)
- **Consequence of This Chapter** (what shifts as a result)
- **Emotional Landing Point** (where the reader leaves her emotionally)
