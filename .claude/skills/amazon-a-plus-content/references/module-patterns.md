# Module Patterns

Use this reference when a user needs deeper genre-specific A+ structure, alternate module options, or a designer-ready brief.

## General A+ Principles

- Lead with the clearest retail promise: genre, tone, story engine, or series identity.
- Keep text readable on mobile. Use fewer words, larger type, strong contrast, and simple focal points.
- Make the cover feel like the source of the page's visual language. Echo palette, typography mood, lighting, texture, symbols, and setting cues.
- Avoid repeating the blurb in full. A+ should dramatize select sales points, not duplicate the description field.
- Use series banners only when they create confidence or read-through desire. For first-in-series or standalone books, use premise, trope, setting, or character promise first.
- Use comparison charts when multiple books, formats, or series entry points could help shoppers decide.

## Common Book Module Types

### Series Identity Banner
Best for established series, connected-world fiction, box sets, and author brands.

Purpose: show professionalism, book count, reading order, and tonal consistency.

Designer notes: place covers large enough to inspect; use a background that supports but does not fight the covers; keep author/series name legible.

### Title/Promise Banner
Best for a single featured book.

Purpose: reinforce title, trope, hook, or central danger/desire.

Designer notes: use one strong background image; make the phrase short; avoid turning it into a second cover.

### Stakes/Premise Panel
Best for thrillers, mysteries, romantic suspense, fantasy, and high-concept fiction.

Purpose: state the problem and consequence in a punchier form than the blurb.

Copy pattern: "When [inciting pressure], [protagonist] must [action] before [consequence]."

### Character Promise Panel
Best for romance, cozy mystery, series fiction, and character-led suspense.

Purpose: sell the lead, couple, sleuth, found family, or recurring cast.

Copy pattern: "She wants [desire]. He hides [secret]. Neither expects [emotional turn]."

### World/Setting Panel
Best for atmospheric fiction, fantasy, historical, small town romance, cozy mystery, and destination suspense.

Purpose: make the location feel like a reason to buy.

Designer notes: use setting-specific imagery, maps, objects, landmarks, interiors, weather, or texture.

### Trope/Reader-Promise Panel
Best for romance, cozy, romantasy, fantasy, and trope-forward genres.

Purpose: help the right reader self-select fast.

Examples: second chance, grumpy/sunshine, locked-room mystery, small-town secrets, heist crew, secret baby, wounded hero, amateur sleuth.

### Social Proof Panel
Best when strong proof exists.

Purpose: borrow trust from reviews, awards, bestseller tags, editorial praise, or author platform.

Rule: do not invent or overstate proof. Keep quotes short and attributable when used.

### Product/Format Panel
Best for box sets, hardcovers, illustrated books, workbooks, nonfiction, and special editions.

Purpose: show what the buyer receives.

Designer notes: include ebook, paperback, hardcover, or bundle mockups only when accurate.

## Genre Defaults

### Psychological Thriller / Crime / Suspense

Visual language: cinematic shadows, neon/night lighting, isolated locations, surveillance, water reflections, doorways, roads, evidence, documents, city texture, storm or fog.

Strong module order:
1. Series or author-brand banner
2. Featured book title/stakes banner
3. Protagonist pressure panel
4. Threat/escalation panel
5. Setting/atmosphere panel
6. Series read-through or product mockup

Copy style: urgent, concrete, low on adjectives. Favor danger, secrets, betrayal, time pressure, and impossible choices.

### Police Procedural / Detective Mystery

Visual language: city at night, evidence boards, caution tape, case files, precinct or street texture, stark contrast.

Strong module order:
1. Series banner or detective identity
2. Case hook
3. Investigation obstacle
4. Victim/suspect web or setting
5. Series order/comparison

Copy style: case-driven, credible, tense. Emphasize investigation, corruption, secrets, and escalating danger.

### Cozy Mystery

Visual language: warm community, shop/cafe/bookstore/bakery, charming objects, seasonal cues, light mystery symbols.

Strong module order:
1. Series world banner
2. Sleuth/community panel
3. Puzzle hook
4. Recurring cast/theme panel
5. Reading order or comparison chart

Copy style: inviting but puzzle-forward. Avoid making the murder feel grim or graphic.

### Contemporary Romance

Visual language: couple chemistry, small town or workplace setting, warm light, tactile objects, emotionally suggestive distance/proximity.

Strong module order:
1. Trope/promise banner
2. Couple conflict panel
3. Emotional stakes panel
4. Setting/community panel
5. Series/connected-world panel

Copy style: desire, obstacle, vulnerability, earned HEA. Match heat level and clean/faith positioning.

### Christian / Clean Romance or Fiction

Visual language: warmth, hope, community, restraint, scenic or symbolic imagery.

Strong module order:
1. Emotional promise or setting banner
2. Character wound/desire panel
3. Faith/hope/community panel
4. Series or author promise

Copy style: avoid preachy language unless the brand expects it. Signal hope, redemption, family, forgiveness, and clean emotional satisfaction.

## Designer Brief Checklist

For each module, specify:

- Primary goal
- Copy hierarchy: headline, subhead, body, optional quote
- Image/background concept
- Cover elements to echo
- Product mockups needed
- Mobile readability risk
- Text-safe zones and contrast notes
- Whether copy is final or provisional
