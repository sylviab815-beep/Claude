# Cozy Mystery Outline Template

Guide the user through an interactive, step-by-step process to develop a cozy mystery story idea into a fully detailed chapter outline. This workflow is specifically designed for cozy mysteries and their subgenres (paranormal cozy, culinary cozy, craft cozy, pet cozy, etc.). Every step pauses for user input before proceeding.

## Core Principles

- **One step at a time**: Complete each step fully, present the output, and WAIT for user feedback before moving on. Never skip ahead or combine steps.
- **Market-informed**: Use K-Lytics report data from Google Drive to identify highly marketable tropes and reader expectations for cozy mysteries.
- **Mystery-first**: The whodunit drives the plot. Every chapter must advance the investigation, plant or reveal clues, or deepen suspect tension — even when other subplots (romance, paranormal, community) are also in play.
- **Detail-oriented outlines**: Every chapter in the final outline must include POV character, summary, tone, goal, complication, decision, consequence, feelings, and clue/red herring tracking — all clearly labeled.
- **Fresh and original**: All names, places, and titles must avoid overused AI-generated patterns. See the Names & Places Avoidance List instructions below.

## Names & Places Avoidance Rules

ALL generated content must avoid overused AI-generated names and patterns. See the detailed avoidance rules in `references/workflow-steps.md` under "Names & Places to Avoid." This applies throughout the ENTIRE workflow — every name, place, title, and business must feel original and real, not AI-generated.

## Number & Time Diversity (Anti-7 Bias)

LLMs default to 7 for "random" numbers. Actively avoid this pattern in all generated content — ages, times, dates, quantities, addresses, durations. Distribute numbers across all ending digits. After completing any outline, scan for the digit 7 and replace most instances with other numbers.

### Chapter Outline Format
Each chapter entry must include:
- **Chapter Number & Title**
- **POV Character**
- **Chapter Summary** (3-5 sentences minimum)
- **Mystery Beat** (where we are in the investigation — discovery, clue gathering, suspect interviewing, red herring, revelation, confrontation, etc.)
- **Clue or Red Herring Planted/Revealed** (specifically what the reader learns or is misdirected by)
- **Suspect Focus** (which suspect is in the spotlight this chapter, if any)
- **Trope Tags** (clearly label which tropes this chapter furthers)
- **Chapter Tone**
- **Chapter Goal** (what this chapter accomplishes for the story)
- **Complication** (what goes wrong or creates tension)
- **Character Decision/Choice** (what the POV character decides)
- **Consequence of That Choice** (what results from that decision)
- **POV Character's Feelings** (emotional state through the chapter)
