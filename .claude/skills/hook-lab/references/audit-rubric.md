# Hook Audit Rubric (reference)

Score a hook on six points, 0–2 each, for a total out of 12. Score what's on the
page, not the author's intent.

| # | Criterion | 0 (fails) | 1 (partial) | 2 (strong) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Hook type clarity** | Not recognizably any of the four types | Leans a type but muddled | Clearly one (or a clean blend) of the four |
| 2 | **Show vs. tell** | Describes the image/book; pure telling | Mixes telling and showing | Shows — adds intrigue the image can't |
| 3 | **Specificity** | Generic; fits half the genre | Somewhat specific | Specific to this book; couldn't be swapped onto another |
| 4 | **Accessibility** | Needs context: unknown names / jargon | One mild snag | Instantly legible; roles not unfamiliar proper nouns |
| 5 | **One-breath read** | Needs 2+ breaths / too long | Slightly long | Reads cleanly in one breath |
| 6 | **Book match** | Promises a different book (pace/heat/tone) | Roughly aligned | Promise fits the real reading experience |

## Verdict bands

- **10–12 — Scroll-stopper.** Ship it; maybe test one variation.
- **7–9 — Close.** One or two fixable issues; rewrite the weak line.
- **4–6 — Weak.** Right instinct, wrong execution; rebuild around a clear type.
- **0–3 — Not a hook yet.** Usually telling or a sales pitch; start over.

## How to use it

1. Read the hook against `hook-framework.md` first.
2. Assign each criterion 0/1/2 and total it.
3. State the score, the verdict band, and the **single biggest problem first**.
4. For every point below 2, quote the offending words and name the issue in one
   sentence (e.g. *"'everything changes' — generic, fits any book → point 3"*).
5. Provide 2–3 rewrites, each labeled with its hook type, each scoring 10+.

## Common deductions (quick reference)

- "Couldn't put it down," "everything changes," "a thrilling ride" → point 3 (generic)
- "Join the adventure in…", "A tale of…" → point 2 (telling) + often point 1
- "Download now / On sale / Grab your copy" in the hook line → point 2 + point 6 (sales pitch)
- Three+ unfamiliar names → point 4
- Two full sentences crammed on the creative → point 5
- A breakneck-thriller hook on a cozy/slow-burn book → point 6
