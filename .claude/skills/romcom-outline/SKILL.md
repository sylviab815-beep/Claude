# Romantic Comedy Outline

Guide the user through an interactive, step-by-step process to develop a romantic comedy from a seed idea into a fully detailed chapter outline. Every step pauses for user input before proceeding.

Rom-com has one job: make the reader laugh AND fall in love. The comedy and the romance must work together — the funniest scenes should also be the most romantic, and the most romantic moments should still have humor woven through them. A rom-com that stops being funny to be romantic has failed. A rom-com that's funny but never makes you root for the couple has also failed.

## Market Context (From K-Lytics June 2025)

This skill is informed by current market data:
- **Diverse Romantic Comedy** is the top-performing category in clean romance: 973 sales/day, Index Score 100 — the benchmark everything else is measured against
- **75% of total royalties** in the contemporary sector concentrate in rom-com
- **Multicultural and diverse protagonists** are highly visible in the top performers — Black leads, multicultural pairings, and inclusive casting are market signals, not trends
- **Illustrated/cartoon covers** are now the genre standard — signal "sweet and modern" to browsers
- **Contemporary settings** dominate (72% of Top 500) — keep it current and relatable
- **Story-heavy elements** (found family, friendship, community) differentiate top performers from the crowded sweet romance field

Use this data to inform story ideas and trope selection throughout the workflow.

## Core Principles

- **One step at a time**: Complete each step fully, present the output, and WAIT for user feedback before moving on. Never skip ahead.
- **Comedy is structural, not decorative**: The funniest moments come from character and situation, not from jokes layered on top. Build the comedy into the premise and the character dynamics.
- **Market-informed**: Use K-Lytics data (if provided) or the market context above to guide trope and casting choices.
- **Fresh and original**: All names, places, and titles must avoid overused AI patterns. Read the bundled Names & Places Avoidance List before generating any content.
- **Diversity as default**: Contemporary rom-com's top performers feature diverse and inclusive casting. Don't default to generic white protagonists — bring real cultural specificity to characters.

## Output Formats

### Character Bio Format (for Novelcrafter)
```
Name:
Age:
Occupation:
Physical Description:
Personality Traits:
Backstory:
Internal Conflict:
External Goal:
Comedic Role (what makes them funny — their flaw, their obliviousness, their defensiveness?):
Relationship to Other Characters:
Arc/Growth:
Quirks/Habits:
Voice/Speech Patterns:
```

### Supporting Character Bio Format
```
Name:
Role in Story:
Relationship to Main Characters:
Key Personality Traits:
Brief Backstory:
Purpose in Plot:
Comedic Function (are they a straight man? A chaos agent? A well-meaning meddler?):
```

### Chapter Outline Format
Each chapter must include:
- **Chapter Number & Title**
- **POV Character**
- **Chapter Summary** (3-5 sentences minimum)
- **Beat Tags** (Comedy / Romance / Both — and which specific beats this chapter delivers)
- **Chapter Tone**
- **Chapter Goal**
- **The Funny** (what's the comedic element — situation, misunderstanding, character flaw on display?)
- **The Romantic** (what advances the emotional connection?)
- **Complication**
- **Character Decision/Choice**
- **Consequence**
- **POV Character's Feelings**
