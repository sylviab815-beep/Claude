# Holiday Romance Author

## Quick Start

This skill writes complete holiday romance manuscripts from finished outlines. Upload your outline, answer 8 setup questions about holiday, heat level, and style, and the system writes your entire book chapter by chapter with the Holiday Integration Engine applied to every chapter.

## What You'll Get

1. Complete manuscript (.docx) — formatted, numbered chapters, proper indentation, all scenes written in full
2. KDP Metadata (.docx) — title, keywords, categories, back-cover copy, launch timing, comp titles

## The Holiday Integration Engine

Every chapter is checked against 5 rules that keep the holiday driving the romance:

1. **Holiday as Plot Engine** — The holiday actively shapes events, forces encounters, and creates situations. It's the driver of action, not decoration.
2. **The Holiday Deadline** — Urgency builds toward the holiday moment. Characters are aware of time running out. Pacing accelerates as the holiday approaches.
3. **Seasonal Emotional Resonance** — Sensory details tied to holiday atmosphere trigger and amplify emotional beats. The holiday's warmth or chill mirrors character emotion.
4. **Holiday-Specific Obstacles** — All obstacles feel organic to THIS specific holiday. Not generic romance problems wearing holiday costumes.
5. **The Holiday Resolution** — The climactic scene uses the holiday moment for maximum emotional impact. The holiday is the stage for the romantic payoff.

## The #1 AI Failure in Holiday Romance

AI weakens holiday romance by treating the holiday as decoration. It generates sensory details that aren't tied to emotion. It creates generic obstacles that have nothing to do with the holiday. It delivers climaxes that could happen at any point in the year. The Holiday Integration Engine prevents this by making the holiday the ENGINE of romance, not the backdrop.

## The Full Process

### Phase 1: Get the Outline
You upload your completed outline. I verify:
- All chapters are listed with scene breakdowns
- Holiday is clearly identified and integrated
- Relationship dynamics are clear
- Heat level is noted
- Holiday Integration Engine is visible in the outline

If incomplete, I ask for the outline builder first.

### Phase 2: Interactive Setup (8 Questions)
1. **Holiday** — Which specific holiday? (Christmas, Halloween, Valentine's, Thanksgiving, New Year's, July 4th, Easter, Cinco de Mayo, Hanukkah, Diwali, other?)
2. **Subgenre** — Contemporary, small-town, second-chance, workplace, stranded/forced proximity, paranormal, fantasy, found family?
3. **Heat Level** — Sweet/Moderate/Steamy/Scorching?
4. **Relationship Type & POV** — MF/FF/MM/Multi/Non-binary? First-person alternating / Third-person alternating / Single POV?
5. **Tense** — Present or Past?
6. **Target Word Count** — Novella (20K-50K) / Standard (70K-90K) / Extended (90K-130K)?
7. **Holiday Sensory Customization** — Any specific details or regional variations for this holiday?
8. **Any Specific Requests** — Avoid certain tropes? Character professions? Names to avoid? Specific scenes to deepen?

### Phase 3: Pre-Writing Prep
- Load current Names Avoidance List (40+ generic names + 30+ holiday-specific names)
- Cross-reference all character names; replace any matches
- Build Holiday Sensory Palette for THIS specific holiday (comprehensive sensory list)
- Create Running Holiday Motif Tracker (recurring holiday details that evolve across chapters)
- Confirm all references loaded and ready
- Set up output folder in your working directory

### Phase 4: Chapter-by-Chapter Writing
For each chapter:
- Write full chapter following outline's scene breakdown
- IMMEDIATELY check Holiday Integration Engine (all 5 rules)
- Check prose-craft rules 1-10 (sensory immersion, emotional mirroring, ticking clock, traditions, food/drink, weather, community, memory, gesture, pacing)
- Update Running Holiday Motif Tracker (mark evolutions, callbacks)
- Log any guardrail interventions
- Move to next chapter

No skipped scenes. No placeholders. Every scene written in full.

### Phase 5: Editorial Pipeline (5 Stages)

**Stage 1: AI-ism Revision**
Strip universal AI crutches plus 15 holiday-romance-specific ones:
- Universal: "a sense of," "couldn't help but," "something shifted," "the air between them," etc.
- Holiday-specific: "the magic of [holiday]," "like something out of a Hallmark movie," "holiday spirit," "festive atmosphere," "winter wonderland," "love was the best gift," "the real meaning of [holiday]," "miracle," "twinkle in their eye," "magic was in the air," etc.

**Stage 2: Developmental Edit**
Structure assessment across 10 dimensions:
- Holiday Integration Compliance — All 5 rules consistently applied?
- Deadline Urgency Arc — Does pressure build toward the holiday?
- Sensory Palette Consistency — Are sensory details from the established palette present?
- Obstacle Authenticity — Do obstacles feel organic to this specific holiday?
- Romantic Tension vs. Holiday Tension — Are these pulling together?
- Character Voice Consistency — Do both leads have distinct, consistent voices?
- Emotional Escalation — Does emotional investment rise with relationship and approaching holiday?
- Pacing Against Holiday Arc — Does early = preparation? Middle = complications? Late = climax?
- Community Feeling — Is the setting/community vivid and immersive?
- Grand Gesture Authenticity — Is the climactic gesture earned and holiday-specific?

**Stage 3: Holiday Authenticity Check**
- Weather/seasonal timing accurate for the date range?
- Holiday traditions portrayed correctly?
- Cultural sensitivity review (non-Christian holidays handled with respect)
- Regional variations respected?

**Stage 4: Beta Read Simulation**
Rate as a holiday romance reader. Provide:
- 3 things that work really well
- 3 things that could be stronger
- 1-2 reader satisfaction questions

**Stage 5: Targeted Revision**
Apply top 3 structural improvements identified across stages 2-4.

### Phase 6: KDP Metadata & Delivery
Generate metadata per KDP standards. Deliver manuscript .docx + metadata .docx to your working directory.

## The 10 Holiday Romance Prose Rules

Every chapter is checked against these craft standards:

1. **Sensory Immersion by Holiday** — Each holiday has its own sensory palette. Prose must LIVE in these senses.
2. **Holiday Atmosphere as Emotional Mirror** — Sensory details tied to emotional beats. Holiday warmth when character is vulnerable; holiday irony when character is conflicted.
3. **The Ticking Clock in Prose** — Time markers tied to the holiday create urgency. Pacing accelerates as deadline approaches.
4. **Holiday Traditions as Intimacy Builders** — Sharing traditions = vulnerability. Teaching traditions = trust.
5. **Food and Drink as Love Language** — Holiday-specific food is never just food. It's connection made tangible.
6. **Weather as Romance Tool** — Snow/rain/cold/fog creates proximity, escalates tension, reflects emotion.
7. **Community and Belonging** — Setting/community feels vivid and immersive. Reader wants to visit this place during this holiday.
8. **Internal Monologue and Holiday Memories** — Characters think about past holidays. Good memories they want to recreate; bad ones they're healing from.
9. **The Grand Holiday Gesture** — Climactic gesture tied to the holiday's timing and meaning. Not generic romance, but holiday-specific romance.
10. **Pacing: The Holiday Build** — Pacing mirrors holiday anticipation. Early chapters = preparation. Middle = complications. Late = holiday itself (fast, emotional, climactic).

## The Anti-7 Bias List

Prevents 7 specific AI failure patterns in holiday romance:

1. **Holiday Disappearance at Midpoint** — The holiday fades from Act 2. Feels like generic romance again.
2. **Sensory Details Disconnected from Emotion** — "The Christmas lights were beautiful. She felt sad." (Stated separately, not tied together)
3. **Generic Obstacles with Holiday Costumes** — Problems that could happen any time of year, just set during holidays
4. **Climax That Doesn't Use the Holiday** — Romance resolves, but the holiday moment is incidental to it
5. **Secondary Characters as One-Off Plot Devices** — Community exists only to move the plot, not to feel lived-in
6. **Holiday Traditions as Decoration, Not Connection** — Traditions mentioned but not used to build intimacy
7. **Pacing That Ignores Holiday Deadline** — No sense of time pressure. Holiday could be removed without changing pacing

## FAQ

**Q: Do I need an outline?**
A: Yes. Completed outline from a holiday romance outline builder. Not a concept, not a partial outline. All chapters, scene breakdowns, clear holiday integration.

**Q: What if I don't have an outline?**
A: Use a holiday romance outline builder first. It guides you through planning with holiday integration built in.

**Q: What if the outline changes?**
A: Let me know before Phase 4 starts. After Phase 4 begins, changes require rewriting affected chapters.

**Q: How long does it take?**
A: Novella 2-4 hours, Standard 5-8 hours, Extended 8-12 hours.

**Q: Will the manuscript be perfect?**
A: It will be professional-quality and ready for KDP or a professional formatter. All 5 Holiday Integration Engine rules applied throughout. Top 3 structural issues fixed. No AI patterns or clichés.

**Q: What about names?**
A: I check your character names against the built-in Names Avoidance List (40+ generic names + 30+ holiday-specific names). Any matches are replaced with fresh alternatives.

**Q: Is the manuscript formatted for KDP?**
A: Yes. Times New Roman 12pt, 1.5 line spacing, 0.5" indent, proper chapter headers, scene breaks, title page, copyright page, TOC, back matter. Ready to upload or send to formatter.

**Q: Can I request changes during writing?**
A: Yes. You can request pauses between phases to review, or request changes (replace character, adjust outline, add scenes). System regenerates from that point forward.
