## Part 1: Title & Subtitle

### Title Guidelines

**What Works in Holiday Romance:**
- Holiday name in title signals to readers
- Signals both genre (romance) and niche (holiday romance)
- Should be compelling and searchable
- 50-75 characters (including spaces) is ideal

**Title Formula Examples:**

```
[Holiday] [Relationship Dynamic] Romance

A [Holiday] [Love Story Type]

[Character Descriptor] [Holiday] [Emotional Promise]
```

**Example Titles (Real Holiday Romance Bestsellers):**
- A Christmas Reunion
- The Christmas Rescue (Suspense + Holiday)
- Halloween Hearts
- A New Year's Second Chance
- Valentine's Day Salvation
- Thanksgiving at the [Location]
- Mistletoe Magic (literally uses cliché, but it works because it's straightforward)

**Title Checklist:**
- ✓ Holiday is in the title (signals the niche)
- ✓ One of these words appears: Love, Romance, Heart, Chance, Reunion, Promise, Return, Rescue, Second Chance
- ✓ Readable and memorable
- ✓ Not overly long (aim for 4-7 words)
- ✓ Specific enough to stand out in search results

**Titles to Avoid:**
- ✗ Generic romance titles that hide the holiday (readers won't find you in holiday searches)
- ✗ Overly clever titles that obscure the genre
- ✗ Titles with typos or unclear meanings

### Subtitle Guidelines (Optional)

**When to use a subtitle:**
- If main title is short and needs context
- If you want to clarify the niche or heat level
- If you want to create a series signal

**Subtitle Examples:**
- Main: "A Christmas Reunion"
  Subtitle: "A Second Chance Holiday Romance"

- Main: "Halloween Hearts"
  Subtitle: "Love Blooms in October"

- Main: "New Year's Resolution"
  Subtitle: "A Sweet Contemporary Romance"

**Subtitle Formula:**
```
[Holiday] [Niche/Trope] [Heat Level Hint]
```

## Part 3: Keywords (7 Required)

Keywords help readers find your book in Amazon search. Holiday romance keywords are crucial for visibility.

### Keyword Strategy for Holiday Romance

**Always include:**
1. The holiday name (e.g., "Christmas romance," "Halloween romance")
2. A descriptor of the trope (e.g., "second-chance romance," "forced proximity")
3. Heat level or tone signal (e.g., "sweet romance," "steamy romance")
4. Setting type (e.g., "small town romance," "contemporary romance")
5. A broader genre signal (e.g., "contemporary romance," "holiday fiction")

**Keyword Formula:**
```
[Holiday] romance, [Trope] romance, [Heat level] romance, [Setting] romance, contemporary romance, [Niche] romance, [Season/Holiday reference]
```

### Keyword Best Practices

- ✓ Include the holiday name (top priority for discoverability)
- ✓ Use "romance" in most keywords (signals genre)
- ✓ Be specific to your subgenre/trope
- ✓ Use multiple variations (e.g., "Christmas romance," "holiday romance," "festive romance")

**Avoid:**

- ✗ Punctuation in keywords (except hyphens)
- ✗ Keywords longer than 5 words typically
- ✗ Unrelated keywords (e.g., "paranormal" if not paranormal romance)

## Part 4: Back-Cover Copy (Book Description)

**Length:** 150-200 words (Amazon allows up to 3,000, but keep it to 150-200 for impact)

**Structure:**
1. Hook (1-2 sentences) — What's the core conflict?
2. Setup (3-4 sentences) — Who are the characters? What's the situation?
3. Stakes (2-3 sentences) — What's at risk? Why does the holiday matter?
4. Call-to-action (1 sentence) — Emotional promise or question

### Back-Cover Copy Formula

```
[Emotional Hook about the holiday or conflict]

[Character A] [situation/background]. [Character B] [situation/background].
When [holiday/circumstance] brings them together, [what happens]. But
[obstacle], and [higher stakes].

Can they [emotional question] before [holiday deadline]?

[Tone shift to emotional promise or call-to-action]
```

```

Christmas Eve, fate—and a stranded car—brings her back to the place she
fled, and the man she loved and lost.

Marcus never got over losing Sophia when she left for the city five years
ago. Running the family bookstore was a reliable heartbreak, a daily
reminder of what he couldn't have. Now she's back, and Christmas magic is

big-city life.

Can they find their way back to each other before the New Year arrives?

From bestselling author [name], a second-chance Christmas romance about
```

```
As they hunt the darkness together, something unexpected blooms between
```

### Back-Cover Copy Checklist

- ✓ Opens with compelling hook (emotion + conflict)
- ✓ Clearly identifies both protagonists
- ✓ Mentions the holiday and why it matters
- ✓ Hints at obstacles without spoiling
- ✓ Raises emotional stakes
- ✓ Ends with question or promise
- ✓ Includes heat level signal (sweet/steamy) if appropriate
- ✓ 150-200 words (count in KDP)
- ✓ No typos or formatting issues
- ✓ Reads like marketing copy, not summary

## Part 8: Publishing Timeline for Holiday Romance

**Critical Timing Strategy:**

Holiday romance readers shop **seasonally**. Publishing timing directly impacts sales.

### Publishing Timeline by Holiday

**Christmas Romances:**
- **Ideal Launch:** October 1 - November 1
- **Why:** Readers holiday shop September-November
- **Last Chance:** December 1 (but sales drop significantly after Dec 15)
- **Avoid:** Launch after January 1 (readers move on)

**Halloween Romances:**
- **Ideal Launch:** August 1 - September 1
- **Why:** Readers prepare for October
- **Last Chance:** September 15
- **Avoid:** Launch after October 1 (seasonal moment passed)

**Valentine's Day Romances:**
- **Ideal Launch:** December 1 - January 15
- **Why:** Romance readers holiday shop; planning Valentine's season
- **Last Chance:** January 31
- **Avoid:** Launch after February 1 (holiday moment passed)

**Thanksgiving Romances:**
- **Ideal Launch:** August 15 - September 15
- **Why:** Readers prepare for fall/holiday season
- **Last Chance:** September 30
- **Avoid:** Launch after October 15 (seasonal moment passed)

**New Year's Eve Romances:**
- **Ideal Launch:** October 15 - December 1
- **Why:** Readers plan for New Year; holiday shopping season
- **Last Chance:** December 31
- **Avoid:** Launch after January 1 (holiday moment passed)

### Pro Tips for Holiday Publishing

- Plan launches 2-3 months before the holiday
- Use the pre-order feature (KDP allows 90-day pre-orders)
- Plan promotions for 2-4 weeks before holiday
- Republish holiday titles every year (no copyright issue with your own work)
- Use KDP Select's free promotion days strategically (3-5 days before launch or during peak search season)

## Final Notes on Holiday Romance Metadata

**Holiday romance readers are searching for:**
1. The specific holiday (Christmas, Halloween, etc.)
2. The romance/emotional tone
3. Specific tropes or settings

Your metadata must make all three elements crystal clear. Readers should know immediately:
- What holiday this book is about
- That it's a romance
- What kind of romance (second-chance, paranormal, small-town, steamy, etc.)

If they can't figure this out in under 10 seconds, your metadata needs revision.

**Test Your Metadata:**
1. Read only your title and keywords
2. Can you identify the holiday? The trope? The tone?
3. Would you click on this book if you were a holiday romance reader?

If the answer is "yes" to all three, your metadata is ready for KDP.
